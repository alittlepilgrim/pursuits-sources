# Article 2 Our Vocation to Beatitude in Brief

*1725 The Beatitudes take up and fulfill God's promises from [[abraham|Abraham]] on by ordering them to the Kingdom of heaven. They respond to the desire for happiness that God has placed in the human heart.* ^ccc-1725

*1726 The Beatitudes teach us the final end to which God calls us: the Kingdom, the vision of God, participation in the divine nature, eternal life, filiation, rest in God.* ^ccc-1726

*1727 The beatitude of eternal life is a gratuitous gift of God. It is supernatural, as is the grace that leads us there.* ^ccc-1727

*1728 The Beatitudes confront us with decisive choices concerning earthly goods; they purify our hearts in order to teach us to love God above all things.* ^ccc-1728

*1729 The beatitude of heaven sets the standards for discernment in the use of earthly goods in keeping with the law of God.* ^ccc-1729
