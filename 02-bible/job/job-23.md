# Job, Chapter 23

## Chapter 23

**Job’s Seventh Reply.**

1 Then Job answered and said: ^job-23-1

2 Today especially my complaint is bitter, ^job-23-2

his hand is heavy upon me in my groanings.

3 Would that I knew how to find him, ^job-23-3

that I might come to his dwelling!

4 I would set out my case before him, ^job-23-4

fill my mouth with arguments;

5 I would learn the words he would answer me, ^job-23-5

understand what he would say to me.

6 Would he contend against me with his great power? ^job-23-6

No, he himself would heed me!

7 There an upright man might argue with him, ^job-23-7

and I would once and for all be delivered from my judge.

8 But if I go east, he is not there; ^job-23-8

or west, I cannot perceive him;

9 The north enfolds him, and I cannot catch sight of him; ^job-23-9

The south hides him, and I cannot see him.

10 Yet he knows my way; ^job-23-10

if he tested me, I should come forth like gold.

11 My foot has always walked in his steps; ^job-23-11

I have kept his way and not turned aside.

12 From the commands of his lips I have not departed; ^job-23-12

the words of his mouth I have treasured in my heart.

13 But once he decides, who can contradict him? ^job-23-13

What he desires, that he does.

14 For he will carry out what is appointed for me, ^job-23-14

and many such things he has in store.

15 Therefore I am terrified before him; ^job-23-15

when I take thought, I dread him.

16 For it is God who has made my heart faint, ^job-23-16

the Almighty who has terrified me.

17 Yes, would that I had vanished in darkness, ^job-23-17

hidden by the thick gloom before me.

\* (23:8) Job’s confident desire to confront God (vv. [2] – [7], contrary to his fears in [9:14] – [20] and [13:21] – [27]) gives way to his dark night: God’s absence (vv. [8] – [9]), which also terrifies (vv. [13] – [17]).
