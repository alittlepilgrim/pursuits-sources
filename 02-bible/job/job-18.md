# Job, Chapter 18

### Chapter 18

**Bildad’s Second Speech.**

1 Then Bildad the Shuhite answered and said: ^job-18-1

2 When will you put an end to words? ^job-18-2

Reflect, and then we can have discussion.

3 Why are we accounted like beasts, ^job-18-3

equal to them in your sight?

4 You who tear yourself in your anger— ^job-18-4

shall the earth be neglected on your account

or the rock be moved out of its place?

5 Truly, the light of the wicked is extinguished; ^job-18-5

the flame of his fire casts no light.

6 In his tent light is darkness; ^job-18-6

the lamp above him goes out.

7 His vigorous steps are hemmed in, ^job-18-7

his own counsel casts him down.

8 A net catches him by the feet, ^job-18-8

he wanders into a pitfall.

9 A trap seizes him by the heel, ^job-18-9

a snare lays hold of him.

10 A noose is hidden for him on the ground, ^job-18-10

a netting for him on the path.

11 On every side terrors frighten him; ^job-18-11

they harry him at each step.

12 His strength is famished, ^job-18-12

disaster is ready at his side,

13 His skin is eaten to the limbs, ^job-18-13

the firstborn of Death eats his limbs.

14 He is plucked from the security of his tent; ^job-18-14

and marched off to the king of terrors.

15 Fire lodges in his tent, ^job-18-15

over his abode brimstone is scattered.

16 Below, his roots dry up, ^job-18-16

and above, his branches wither.

17 His memory perishes from the earth, ^job-18-17

and he has no name in the countryside.

18 He is driven from light into darkness, ^job-18-18

and banished from the world.

19 He has neither offshoot nor offspring among his people, ^job-18-19

no survivor where once he dwelt.

20 Those who come after shall be appalled at his fate; ^job-18-20

those who went before are seized with horror.

21 So is it then with the dwelling of the impious; ^job-18-21

such is the place of the one who does not know God!

\* (18:13) Firstborn of Death: that is, disease, plague.

\* (18:14) The king of terrors: of Sheol, of Death (cf. the “terrors” in v. [11]). However, the Hebrew of this verse is obscure.
