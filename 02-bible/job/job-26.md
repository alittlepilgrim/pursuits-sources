# Job, Chapter 26

## Chapter 26

**Job’s Reply.**

1 Then Job answered and said: ^job-26-1

2 What help you give to the powerless, ^job-26-2

what strength to the feeble arm!

3 How you give counsel to one without wisdom; ^job-26-3

how profuse is the advice you offer!

4 With whose help have you uttered those words, ^job-26-4

whose breath comes forth from you?

5 The shades beneath writhe in terror, ^job-26-5

the waters, and their inhabitants.

6 Naked before him is Sheol, ^job-26-6

and Abaddon has no covering.

7 He stretches out Zaphon over the void, ^job-26-7

and suspends the earth over nothing at all;

8 He binds up the waters in his clouds, ^job-26-8

yet the cloud is not split by their weight;

9 He holds back the appearance of the full moon ^job-26-9

by spreading his clouds before it.

10 He has marked out a circle on the surface of the deep ^job-26-10

as the boundary of light and darkness.

11 The pillars of the heavens tremble ^job-26-11

and are stunned at his thunderous rebuke;

12 By his power he stilled Sea, ^job-26-12

by his skill he crushed Rahab;

13 By his wind the heavens were made clear, ^job-26-13

his hand pierced the fleeing serpent.

14 Lo, these are but the outlines of his ways, ^job-26-14

and what a whisper of a word we hear of him:

Who can comprehend the thunder of his power?

\* (26:1] – [14) Perhaps to be read as Job’s reply to Bildad’s short speech.

\* (26:5) Shades: the dead in Sheol, the nether world; cf. [Ps 88:11]; [Is 26:14].

\* (26:6) Sheol: cf. note on ([[psalm-06|Ps 6]]:6]. Abaddon: Hebrew for “(place of) destruction,” a synonym for nether world; cf. [Jb 28:22]; ([[revelation-09|Rv 9]]:11].

\* (26:7) Zaphon: lit., “the north,” used here as a synonym for the firmament, the heavens; cf. [[isaiah-14|Is 14]]:13.

\* (26:10) Circle: the horizon of the ocean which serves as the boundary for the activity of light and darkness; cf. [Prv 8:27].

\* (26:12) Rahab: another name for the primeval sea-monster; see notes on [Jb 3:8] and [Ps 89:11]; cf. also [Jb 7:12]; [9:13].

\* (26:13) The fleeing serpent: the same term occurs in [Is 27:1] in apposition to Leviathan; see note on [Jb 3:8].
