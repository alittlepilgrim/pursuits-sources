# Job, Chapter 34

## Chapter 34

1 Then Elihu answered and said: ^job-34-1

2 Hear my discourse, you that are wise; ^job-34-2

you that have knowledge, listen to me!

3 For the ear tests words, ^job-34-3

as the palate tastes food.

4 Let us choose what is right; ^job-34-4

let us determine among ourselves what is good.

5 For Job has said, “I am innocent, ^job-34-5

but God has taken away what is my right.

6 I declare the judgment on me to be a lie; ^job-34-6

my arrow-wound is incurable, sinless though I am.”

7 What man is like Job? ^job-34-7

He drinks in blasphemies like water,

8 Keeps company with evildoers ^job-34-8

and goes along with the wicked,

9 When he says, “There is no profit ^job-34-9

in pleasing God.”

10 Therefore, you that have understanding, hear me: ^job-34-10

far be it from God to do wickedness;

far from the Almighty to do wrong!

11 Rather, he requites mortals for their conduct, ^job-34-11

and brings home to them their way of life.

12 Surely, God cannot act wickedly, ^job-34-12

the Almighty cannot pervert justice.

13 Who gave him charge over the earth, ^job-34-13

or who set all the world in its place?

14 If he were to set his mind to it, ^job-34-14

gather to himself his spirit and breath,

15 All flesh would perish together, ^job-34-15

and mortals return to dust.

16 Now you—understand, hear this! ^job-34-16

Listen to the words I speak!

17 Can an enemy of justice be in control, ^job-34-17

will you condemn the supreme Just One,

18 Who says to a king, “You are worthless!” ^job-34-18

and to nobles, “You are wicked!”

19 Who neither favors the person of princes, ^job-34-19

nor respects the rich more than the poor?

For they are all the work of his hands;

20 in a moment they die, even at midnight. ^job-34-20

People are shaken, and pass away,

the powerful are removed without lifting a hand;

21 For his eyes are upon our ways, ^job-34-21

and all our steps he sees.

22 There is no darkness so dense ^job-34-22

that evildoers can hide in it.

23 For no one has God set a time ^job-34-23

to come before him in judgment.

24 Without inquiry he shatters the mighty, ^job-34-24

and appoints others in their place,

25 Thus he discerns their works; ^job-34-25

overnight they are crushed.

26 Where the wicked are, he strikes them, ^job-34-26

in a place where all can see,

27 Because they turned away from him ^job-34-27

and did not understand his ways at all:

28 And made the cry of the poor reach him, ^job-34-28

so that he heard the cry of the afflicted.

29 If he is silent, who then can condemn? ^job-34-29

If he hides his face, who then can behold him,

whether nation or individual?

30 Let an impious man not rule, ^job-34-30

nor those who ensnare their people.

31 Should anyone say to God, ^job-34-31

“I accept my punishment; I will offend no more;

32 What I cannot see, teach me: ^job-34-32

if I have done wrong, I will do so no more,”

33 Would you then say that God must punish, ^job-34-33

when you are disdainful?

It is you who must choose, not I;

speak, therefore, what you know.

34 Those who understand will say to me, ^job-34-34

all the wise who hear my views:

35 “Job speaks without knowledge, ^job-34-35

his words make no sense.

36 Let Job be tested to the limit, ^job-34-36

since his answers are those of the impious;

37 For he is adding rebellion to his sin ^job-34-37

by brushing off our arguments

and addressing many words to God.”

\* (34:1) Elihu replies, although no one else has spoken. This connective phrase (see also [35:1] and [36:1]) may indicate that these speeches of Elihu are a secondary addition to the book (see note on [32:2]).

\* (34:16) Now you: Elihu turns to Job and addresses him directly.
