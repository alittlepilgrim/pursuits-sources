# Job, Chapter 31

## Chapter 31

1 I made a covenant with my eyes ^job-31-1

not to gaze upon a virgin.

2 What portion comes from God above, ^job-31-2

what heritage from the Almighty on high?

3 Is it not calamity for the unrighteous, ^job-31-3

and woe for evildoers?

4 Does he not see my ways, ^job-31-4

and number all my steps?

5 If I have walked in falsehood ^job-31-5

and my foot has hastened to deceit,

6 Let God weigh me in the scales of justice; ^job-31-6

thus will he know my innocence!

7 If my steps have turned out of the way, ^job-31-7

and my heart has followed my eyes,

or any stain clings to my hands,

8 Then may I sow, but another eat, ^job-31-8

and may my produce be rooted up!

9 If my heart has been enticed toward a woman, ^job-31-9

and I have lain in wait at my neighbor’s door;

10 Then may my wife grind for another, ^job-31-10

and may others kneel over her!

11 For that would be heinous, ^job-31-11

a crime to be condemned,

12 A fire that would consume down to Abaddon ^job-31-12

till it uprooted all my crops.

13 Had I refused justice to my manservant ^job-31-13

or to my maidservant, when they had a complaint against me,

14 What then should I do when God rises up? ^job-31-14

What could I answer when he demands an account?

15 Did not he who made me in the belly make him? ^job-31-15

Did not the same One fashion us in the womb?

16 If I have denied anything that the poor desired, ^job-31-16

or allowed the eyes of the widow to languish

17 While I ate my portion alone, ^job-31-17

with no share in it for the fatherless,

18 Though like a father he has reared me from my youth, ^job-31-18

guiding me even from my mother’s womb—

19 If I have seen a wanderer without clothing, ^job-31-19

or a poor man without covering,

20 Whose limbs have not blessed me ^job-31-20

when warmed with the fleece of my sheep;

21 If I have raised my hand against the innocent ^job-31-21

because I saw that I had supporters at the gate—

22 Then may my arm fall from the shoulder, ^job-31-22

my forearm be broken at the elbow!

23 For I dread calamity from God, ^job-31-23

and his majesty will overpower me.

24 Had I put my trust in gold ^job-31-24

or called fine gold my security;

25 Or had I rejoiced that my wealth was great, ^job-31-25

or that my hand had acquired abundance—

26 Had I looked upon the light as it shone, ^job-31-26

or the moon in the splendor of its progress,

27 And had my heart been secretly enticed ^job-31-27

to blow them a kiss with my hand,

28 This too would be a crime for condemnation, ^job-31-28

for I should have denied God above.

29 Had I rejoiced at the destruction of my enemy ^job-31-29

or exulted when evil came upon him,

30 Even though I had not allowed my mouth to sin ^job-31-30

by invoking a curse against his life—

31 Had not the men of my tent exclaimed, ^job-31-31

“Who has not been filled with his meat!”

32 No stranger lodged in the street, ^job-31-32

for I opened my door to wayfarers—

33 Had I, all too human, hidden my sins ^job-31-33

and buried my guilt in my bosom

34 Because I feared the great multitude ^job-31-34

and the scorn of the clans terrified me—

then I should have remained silent, and not come out of doors!

35 Oh, that I had one to hear my case: ^job-31-35

here is my signature: let the Almighty answer me!

Let my accuser write out his indictment!

36 Surely, I should wear it on my shoulder ^job-31-36

or put it on me like a diadem;

37 Of all my steps I should give him an account; ^job-31-37

like a prince I should present myself before him.

38 If my land has cried out against me ^job-31-38

till its furrows wept together;

39 If I have eaten its strength without payment ^job-31-39

and grieved the hearts of its tenants;

40 Then let the thorns grow instead of wheat ^job-31-40

and stinkweed instead of barley!

The words of Job are ended.

\* (31:5] – [34) In a series of purificatory oaths, Job protests his innocence.

\* (31:12) Abaddon: see note on [26:6].

\* (31:18) He: presumably God.

\* (31:21) Gate: cf. notes on [5:4]; [Ru 4:1].

\* (31:26] – [28) Light: of the sun. Job never sinned by worshiping the sun or the moon. Blow them a kiss: an act of idolatrous worship.

\* (31:31) The members of his extended family will testify to his hospitality.

\* (31:33] – [34) Job’s present protest is made, not in spite of hidden sins which he had been unwilling to disclose, but out of genuine innocence. All too human: can also be translated “like Adam.”

\* (31:35] – [37) This concluding bravado fits better after v. [40a].

\* (31:35) My signature: lit., “*tau*,” the last letter of the Hebrew alphabet, shaped like a cross. Job issues a subpoena to God, and challenges him to follow proper legal procedure as well.

\* (31:36) On my shoulder: i.e., boldly, proudly.

\* (31:37) Like a prince: not as a frightened criminal.
