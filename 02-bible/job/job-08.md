# Job, Chapter 8

## Chapter 8

**Bildad’s First Speech.**

1 Bildad the Shuhite answered and said: ^job-08-1

2 How long will you utter such things? ^job-08-2

The words from your mouth are a mighty wind!

3 Does God pervert judgment, ^job-08-3

does the Almighty pervert justice?

4 If your children have sinned against him ^job-08-4

and he has left them in the grip of their guilt,

5 Still, if you yourself have recourse to God ^job-08-5

and make supplication to the Almighty,

6 Should you be blameless and upright, ^job-08-6

surely now he will rouse himself for you

and restore your rightful home.

7 Though your beginning was small, ^job-08-7

your future will flourish indeed.

8 Inquire of the former generations, ^job-08-8

pay attention to the experience of their ancestors—

9 As we are but of yesterday and have no knowledge, ^job-08-9

because our days on earth are but a shadow—

10 Will they not teach you and tell you ^job-08-10

and utter their words of understanding?

11 Can the papyrus grow up without mire? ^job-08-11

Can the reed grass flourish without water?

12 While it is yet green and uncut, ^job-08-12

it withers quicker than any grass.

13 So is the end of everyone who forgets God, ^job-08-13

and so shall the hope of the godless perish.

14 His confidence is but a gossamer thread, ^job-08-14

his trust is a spider’s house.

15 He shall lean upon his house, but it shall not stand; ^job-08-15

he shall cling to it, but it shall not endure.

16 He thrives in full sun, ^job-08-16

and over his garden his shoots go forth;

17 About a heap of stones his roots are entwined; ^job-08-17

among the rocks he takes hold.

18 Yet if one tears him from his place, ^job-08-18

it will disown him: “I have never seen you!”

19 There he lies rotting beside the road, ^job-08-19

and out of the soil another sprouts.

20 Behold, God will not cast away the upright; ^job-08-20

neither will he take the hand of the wicked.

21 Once more will he fill your mouth with laughter ^job-08-21

and your lips with rejoicing.

22 Those who hate you shall be clothed with shame, ^job-08-22

and the tent of the wicked shall be no more.

\* (8:11] – [13) As marsh plants need water, so human beings need God. These verses may be a quotation from the teaching of the ancestors; cf. v. [10].
