# Job, Chapter 5

### Chapter 5

1 Call now! Will anyone respond to you? ^job-05-1

To which of the holy ones will you turn?

2 Surely impatience kills the fool ^job-05-2

and indignation slays the simpleton.

3 I have seen a fool spreading his roots, ^job-05-3

but I cursed his household suddenly:

4 May his children be far from safety; ^job-05-4

may they be crushed at the gate without a rescuer.

5 What they have reaped may the hungry eat up, ^job-05-5

or God take away by blight,

or the thirsty swallow their substance.

6 For not from dust does mischief come, ^job-05-6

nor from the soil does trouble sprout.

7 Human beings beget mischief ^job-05-7

as sparks fly upward.

8 In your place, I would appeal to God, ^job-05-8

and to God I would state my plea.

9 He does things great and unsearchable, ^job-05-9

things marvelous and innumerable.

10 He gives rain upon the earth ^job-05-10

and sends water upon the fields;

11 He sets up the lowly on high, ^job-05-11

and those who mourn are raised to safety.

12 He frustrates the plans of the cunning, ^job-05-12

so that their hands achieve no success;

13 He catches the wise in their own ruses, ^job-05-13

and the designs of the crafty are routed.

14 They meet with darkness in the daytime, ^job-05-14

at noonday they grope as though it were night.

15 But he saves the poor from the sword of their mouth, ^job-05-15

from the hand of the mighty.

16 Thus the needy have hope, ^job-05-16

and iniquity closes its mouth.

17 Happy the one whom God reproves! ^job-05-17

The Almighty’s discipline do not reject.

18 For he wounds, but he binds up; ^job-05-18

he strikes, but his hands give healing.

19 Out of six troubles he will deliver you, ^job-05-19

and at the seventh no evil shall touch you.

20 In famine he will deliver you from death, ^job-05-20

and in war from the power of the sword;

21 From the scourge of the tongue you shall be hidden, ^job-05-21

and you shall not fear approaching ruin.

22 At ruin and want you shall laugh; ^job-05-22

the beasts of the earth, do not fear.

23 With the stones of the field shall your covenant be, ^job-05-23

and the wild beasts shall be at peace with you.

24 And you shall know that your tent is secure; ^job-05-24

taking stock of your household, you shall miss nothing.

25 You shall know that your descendants are many, ^job-05-25

and your offspring like the grass of the earth.

26 You shall approach the grave in full vigor, ^job-05-26

as a shock of grain comes in at its season.

27 See, this we have searched out; so it is! ^job-05-27

This we have heard, and you should know.

\* (5:1) Holy ones: members of the heavenly court; cf. [1:6] and note. They were viewed as heavenly intercessors.

\* (5:4) At the gate: of the city, where justice was administered.

\* (5:7) Sparks: in Hebrew, “sons of resheph,” which the ancient versions took as the name of a bird. Resheph was an underworld deity of plague, but the word also means “flames” in [Sg 8:6].

\* (5:9) Perhaps to be omitted here; it is a duplicate of [9:10].

\* (5:15) From the sword of their mouth: the Hebrew is obscure.

\* (5:17) Almighty: standard translation of Heb. *Shaddai*.

\* (5:19) Six…the seventh: proverbial expression for any large number; cf. [Prv 24:16]; ([[luke-17|Lk 17]]:4].
