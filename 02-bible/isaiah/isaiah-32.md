# Isaiah, Chapter 32

## Chapter 32

### The Kingdom of Justice

1 See, a king will reign justly ^isaiah-32-1

and princes will rule rightly.

2 Each of them will be like a shelter from the wind, ^isaiah-32-2

a refuge from the rain.

They will be like streams of water in a dry country,

like the shade of a great rock in a parched land.

3 The eyes of those who see will not be closed; ^isaiah-32-3

the ears of those who hear will be attentive.

4 The hasty of heart shall take thought to know, ^isaiah-32-4

and tongues of stutterers shall speak readily and clearly.

5 No more will the fool be called noble, ^isaiah-32-5

nor the deceiver be considered honorable.

6 For the fool speaks folly, ^isaiah-32-6

his heart plans evil:

Godless actions,

perverse speech against the LORD,

Letting the hungry go empty

and the thirsty without drink.

7 The deceits of the deceiver are evil, ^isaiah-32-7

he plans devious schemes:

To ruin the poor with lies,

and the needy when they plead their case.

8 But the noble plan noble deeds, ^isaiah-32-8

and in noble deeds they persist.

### The Women of Jerusalem

9 You women so complacent, rise up and hear my voice, ^isaiah-32-9

daughters so confident, give heed to my words.

10 In a little more than a year ^isaiah-32-10

your confidence will be shaken;

For the vintage will fail,

no fruit harvest will come in.

11 Tremble, you who are so complacent! ^isaiah-32-11

Shudder, you who are so confident!

Strip yourselves bare,

with only a loincloth for cover.

12 Beat your breasts ^isaiah-32-12

for the pleasant fields,

for the fruitful vine;

13 For the soil of my people, ^isaiah-32-13

overgrown with thorns and briers;

For all the joyful houses,

the exultant city.

14 The castle will be forsaken, ^isaiah-32-14

the noisy city deserted;

Citadel and tower will become wasteland forever,

the joy of wild donkeys, the pasture of flocks;

15 Until the spirit from on high ^isaiah-32-15

is poured out on us.

And the wilderness becomes a garden land

and the garden land seems as common as forest.

16 Then judgment will dwell in the wilderness ^isaiah-32-16

and justice abide in the garden land.

17 The work of justice will be peace; ^isaiah-32-17

the effect of justice, calm and security forever.

18 My people will live in peaceful country, ^isaiah-32-18

in secure dwellings and quiet resting places.

19 And the forest will come down completely, ^isaiah-32-19

the city will be utterly laid low.

20 Happy are you who sow beside every stream, ^isaiah-32-20

and let the ox and the donkey go freely!

\* (32:14) The castle: the fortified royal palace in Jerusalem. Citadel: Ophel, the fortified hill, with its stronghold called “the great projecting tower” ([Neh 3:27]).

\* (32:15] – [18], [20) Extraordinary peace and prosperity will come to Israel under just rulers.

\* (32:19) Probably from a different context, perhaps after v. [14a].
