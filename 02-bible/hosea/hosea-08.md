# Hosea, Chapter 8

## Chapter 8

### Corruption of Cult, Domestic and Foreign Politics

1 Put the trumpet to your lips! ^hosea-08-1

One like an eagle is over the house of the LORD!

Because they have violated my covenant,

and rebelled against my law,

2 They cry out to me, ^hosea-08-2

“My God! We know you!”

3 But Israel has rejected what is good; ^hosea-08-3

the enemy shall pursue him.

4 They made kings, but not by my authority; ^hosea-08-4

they established princes, but without my knowledge.

With their silver and gold

they made idols for themselves,

to their own destruction.

5 He has rejected your calf, Samaria! ^hosea-08-5

My wrath is kindled against them;

How long will they be incapable of innocence

in Israel?

6 An artisan made it, ^hosea-08-6

it is no god at all.

The calf of Samaria

will be dashed to pieces.

7 When they sow the wind, ^hosea-08-7

they will reap the whirlwind;

The stalk of grain that forms no head

can yield no flour;

Even if it could,

strangers would swallow it.

8 Israel is swallowed up; ^hosea-08-8

now they are among the nations,

like a useless vessel.

9 For they went up to Assyria— ^hosea-08-9

a wild ass off on its own—

Ephraim bargained for lovers.

10 Even though they bargain with the nations, ^hosea-08-10

I will now gather them together;

They will soon succumb

under the burden of king and princes.

11 When Ephraim made many altars to expiate sin, ^hosea-08-11

they became altars for sinning.

12 Though I write for him my many instructions, ^hosea-08-12

they are considered like a stranger’s.

13 They love sacrifice, ^hosea-08-13

they sacrifice meat and eat it,

but the LORD is not pleased with them.

Now he will remember their guilt

and punish their sins;

they shall return to Egypt.

14 Israel has forgotten his maker ^hosea-08-14

and has built palaces.

Judah, too, has fortified many cities,

but I will send fire upon his cities,

to devour their strongholds.

\* (8:1) Eagle: perhaps an image for Tiglath-pileser III of Assyria, who overran the land of Israel in 733 B.C. ([Jer 48:40]; [49:22]; [[ezekiel-17|Ez 17]]:3).

\* (8:3) Enemy: Assyria.

\* (8:4) Hosea is not against the monarchy, but against the conspiracies at the royal court (see note on [7:3] – [7]). The king should be chosen by God ([[1-kings-19|1 Kgs 19]]:15 – [16]).

\* (8:5) Calf: a cultic object introduced by Jeroboam I after the separation of the Northern Kingdom from the Southern Kingdom ([[1-kings-12|1 Kgs 12]]:26 – [30]; cf. [Ex 32]).

\* (8:9) They went up to Assyria: a reference to the politics of appealing to Assyria (cf. [5:13]; [7:11]). There is a play on the Hebrew word for “wild ass” (*pere*) and “Ephraim.”

\* (8:10) I will now gather them: for judgment and for deportation.

\* (8:11) The altars had become places of self-serving worship (cf. v. [13]).

\* (8:13) Return to Egypt: to punish their violation of the covenant they will experience a reversal of the exodus.
