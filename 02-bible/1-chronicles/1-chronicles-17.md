# 1 Chronicles, Chapter 17

### Chapter 17

**The Oracle of Nathan.**

1 After David had taken up residence in his house, he said to Nathan the prophet, “See, I am living in a house of cedar, but the ark of the covenant of the L ORD is under tentcloth.” ^1-chronicles-17-1

2 Nathan replied to David, “Whatever is in your heart, go and do, for God is with you.” ^1-chronicles-17-2

3 But that same night the word of God came to Nathan: ^1-chronicles-17-3

4 Go and tell David my servant, Thus says the L ORD: It is not you who are to build the house for me to dwell in. ^1-chronicles-17-4

5 For I have never dwelt in a house, from the day I brought Israel up, even to this day, but I have been lodging in tent or tabernacle. ^1-chronicles-17-5

6 As long as I have wandered about with all Israel, did I ever say a word to any of the judges of Israel whom I commanded to shepherd my people, Why have you not built me a house of cedar? ^1-chronicles-17-6

7 Now then, speak thus to my servant David, Thus says the L ORD of hosts: I took you from the pasture, from following the flock, to become ruler over my people Israel. ^1-chronicles-17-7

8 I was with you wherever you went, and I cut down all your enemies before you. I will make your name like that of the greatest on the earth. ^1-chronicles-17-8

9 I will assign a place for my people Israel and I will plant them in it to dwell there; they will never again be disturbed, nor shall the wicked ever again oppress them, as they did at the beginning, ^1-chronicles-17-9

10 and during all the time when I appointed judges over my people Israel. And I will subdue all your enemies. Moreover, I declare to you that the L ORD will build you a house: ^1-chronicles-17-10

11 when your days have been completed and you must join your ancestors, I will raise up your offspring after you who will be one of your own sons, and I will establish his kingdom. ^1-chronicles-17-11

12 He it is who shall build me a house, and I will establish his throne forever. ^1-chronicles-17-12

13 I will be a father to him, and he shall be a son to me, and I will not withdraw my favor from him as I withdrew it from the one who was before you; ^1-chronicles-17-13

14 but I will maintain him in my house and in my kingdom forever, and his throne shall be firmly established forever. ^1-chronicles-17-14

15 In accordance with all these words and this whole vision Nathan spoke to David. ^1-chronicles-17-15

**David’s Thanksgiving.**

16 Then King David came in and sat in the L ORD’s presence, and said: “Who am I, L ORD God, and what is my house, that you should have brought me so far? ^1-chronicles-17-16

17 And yet, even this is too little in your sight, O God! For you have made a promise regarding your servant’s house reaching into the future, and you have looked on me as henceforth the most notable of men, L ORD God. ^1-chronicles-17-17

18 What more can David say to you? You have known your servant. ^1-chronicles-17-18

19 L ORD, for your servant’s sake and in keeping with your purpose, you have done this great thing. ^1-chronicles-17-19

20 L ORD, there is no one like you, no God but you, just as we have always heard. ^1-chronicles-17-20

21 “Is there, like your people Israel, whom you redeemed from Egypt, another nation on earth whom a god went to redeem as his people? You won for yourself a name for great and awesome deeds by driving out the nations before your people. ^1-chronicles-17-21

22 You made your people Israel your own forever, and you, L ORD, became their God. ^1-chronicles-17-22

23 Now, L ORD, may the promise that you have spoken concerning your servant and his house remain firm forever. Bring about what you have promised, ^1-chronicles-17-23

24 that your name, L ORD of hosts, God of Israel, may be great and abide forever, while the house of your servant is established in your presence. ^1-chronicles-17-24

25 “Because you, my God, have revealed to your servant that you will build him a house, your servant dares to pray before you. ^1-chronicles-17-25

26 Since you, L ORD, are truly God and have made this generous promise to your servant, ^1-chronicles-17-26

27 do, then, bless the house of your servant, that it may be in your presence forever—since it is you, L ORD, who blessed it, it is blessed forever.” ^1-chronicles-17-27
