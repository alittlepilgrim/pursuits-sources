# 1 Chronicles, Chapter 25

### Chapter 25

**The Singers.**

1 David and the leaders of the liturgy set apart for the service the sons of Asaph, Heman, and Jeduthun, who prophesied to the accompaniment of lyres and harps and cymbals. ^1-chronicles-25-1

This is the list of those who performed this service:

3 Of Jeduthun, these sons of Jeduthun: Gedaliah, Zeri, Jeshaiah, Shimei, Hashabiah, and Mattithiah; six, under the direction of their father Jeduthun, who prophesied to the accompaniment of a lyre, to give thanks and praise to the L ORD. ^1-chronicles-25-3

4 Of Heman, these sons of Heman: Bukkiah, Mattaniah, Uzziel, Shubael, and Jerimoth; Hananiah, Hanani, Eliathah, Giddalti, Romamti-ezer, Joshbekashah, Mallothi, Hothir, and Mahazioth. ^1-chronicles-25-4

5 All these were the sons of Heman, the king’s seer for divine matters; to exalt him God gave Heman fourteen sons and three daughters. ^1-chronicles-25-5

6 All these, whether of Asaph, Jeduthun, or Heman, were under their fathers’ direction in the singing in the house of the L ORD to the accompaniment of cymbals, harps and lyres, serving in the house of God, under the guidance of the king. ^1-chronicles-25-6

7 Their number, together with that of their kinsmen who were trained in singing to the L ORD, all of them skilled men, was two hundred and eighty-eight. ^1-chronicles-25-7

8 They cast lots for their functions equally, young and old, master and pupil alike. ^1-chronicles-25-8

9 The first lot fell to Asaph, to the family of Joseph; he and his sons and his kinsmen were twelve. Gedaliah was the second; he and his kinsmen and his sons were twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-9

10 The third was Zaccur, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-10

11 The fourth fell to Izri, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-11

12 The fifth was Nethaniah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-12

13 The sixth was Bukkiah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-13

14 The seventh was Jesarelah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-14

15 The eighth was Jeshaiah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-15

16 The ninth was Mattaniah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-16

17 The tenth was Shimei, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-17

18 The eleventh was Uzziel, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-18

19 The twelfth fell to Hashabiah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-19

20 The thirteenth was Shubael, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-20

21 The fourteenth was Mattithiah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-21

22 The fifteenth fell to Jeremoth, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-22

23 The sixteenth fell to Hananiah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-23

24 The seventeenth fell to Joshbekashah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-24

25 The eighteenth fell to Hanani, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-25

26 The nineteenth fell to Mallothi, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-26

27 The twentieth fell to Eliathah, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-27

28 The twenty-first fell to Hothir, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-28

29 The twenty-second fell to Giddalti, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-29

30 The twenty-third fell to Mahazioth, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-30

31 The twenty-fourth fell to Romamti-ezer, his sons, and his kinsmen: twelve. ^1-chronicles-25-31

\* \[[25:1]\] This list of twenty-four classes of Temple singers balances the list of the twenty-four classes of priests ([24:4] – [19]). The last nine names in v. [4], which seem to form a special group, were perhaps originally fragments or incipits (the opening words) of hymns. With some slight changes in the vocalization, these names would mean: “Have mercy on me, O L ORD,” “Have mercy on me,” “You are my God,” “I magnify,” “I extol the help of…,” “Sitting in adversity,” “I have fulfilled,” “He made abundant,” and “Visions.”
