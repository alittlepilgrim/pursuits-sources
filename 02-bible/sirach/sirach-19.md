# Sirach, Chapter 19

## Chapter 19

1 Whoever does this grows no richer; ^sirach-19-1

those who waste the little they have will be stripped bare.

2 Wine and women make the heart lustful, ^sirach-19-2

and the companion of prostitutes becomes reckless.

3 Rottenness and worms will possess him, ^sirach-19-3

and the reckless will be snatched away.

4 Whoever trusts others too quickly has a shallow mind, ^sirach-19-4

and those who sin wrong themselves.

### The Proper Use of Speech

5 Whoever gloats over evil will be destroyed, ^sirach-19-5

6 and whoever repeats gossip has no sense. ^sirach-19-6

7 Never repeat gossip, ^sirach-19-7

and no one will reproach you.

8 Tell nothing to friend or foe; ^sirach-19-8

and unless it be a sin for you, do not reveal a thing.

9 For someone may have heard you and watched you, ^sirach-19-9

and in time come to hate you.

10 Let anything you hear die with you; ^sirach-19-10

never fear, it will not make you burst!

11 Having heard something, the fool goes into labor, ^sirach-19-11

like a woman giving birth to a child.

12 Like an arrow stuck in a fool’s thigh, ^sirach-19-12

so is gossip in the belly of a fool.

13 Admonish your friend—he may not have done it; ^sirach-19-13

and if he did, that he may not do it again.

14 Admonish your neighbor—he may not have said it; ^sirach-19-14

and if he did, that he may not say it again.

15 Admonish your friend—often it may be slander; ^sirach-19-15

do not believe every story.

16 Then, too, a person can slip and not mean it; ^sirach-19-16

who has not sinned with his tongue?

17 Admonish your neighbor before you break with him; ^sirach-19-17

and give due place to the Law of the Most High.

### How to Recognize True Wisdom

20 All wisdom is fear of the LORD; ^sirach-19-20

and in all wisdom, the observance of the Law.

22 The knowledge of wickedness is not wisdom, ^sirach-19-22

nor is there prudence in the counsel of sinners.

23 There is a shrewdness that is detestable, ^sirach-19-23

while the fool may be free from sin.

24 Better are the God-fearing who have little understanding ^sirach-19-24

than those of great intelligence who violate the Law.

25 There is a shrewdness keen but dishonest, ^sirach-19-25

and there are those who are duplicitous to win a judgment.

26 There is the villain bowed in grief, ^sirach-19-26

but full of deceit within.

27 He hides his face and pretends not to hear, ^sirach-19-27

but when not observed, he will take advantage of you:

28 Even if his lack of strength keeps him from sinning, ^sirach-19-28

when he finds the right time he will do harm.

29 People are known by their appearance; ^sirach-19-29

the sensible are recognized as such when first met.

30 One’s attire, hearty laughter, and gait ^sirach-19-30

proclaim him for what he is.

\* (19:5] – [17) An excellent commentary on bearing false witness (([[exodus-20|Ex 20]]:16]; [Dt 5:20]). Ben Sira speaks harshly about calumny, rash judgment, and detraction (vv. [5] – [7]), and urges discreet silence (vv. [8] – [12]). Justice requires that an accused neighbor be given a hearing, and charity urges fraternal correction; both together fulfill the law of the Most High (vv. [13] – [17]); cf. ([[matthew-07|Mt 7]]:1] – [2]; [18:15] – [16].

† (19:17) Other ancient texts read as vv. 18–19:

<sup>18</sup> Fear of the Lord is the beginning of acceptance;

and wisdom from him obtains love.

<sup>19</sup> Knowledge of the Lord’s commandments is life-giving instruction;

those who do what pleases him will harvest the fruit of the tree of immortality.

\* (19:20] – [30) True wisdom is contrasted with a dishonest shrewdness.

† (19:20) Other ancient texts read as v. 21:

The slave who says to his master, “What pleases you I will not do”—

even if he does it later, provokes the one who feeds him.
