# Sirach, Chapter 32

## Chapter 32

1 If you are chosen to preside at a dinner, do not be puffed up, ^sirach-32-1

but with the guests be as one of them;

Take care of them first and then sit down;

2 see to their needs, and then take your place, ^sirach-32-2

To share in their joy

and receive a wreath for a job well done.

3 You who are older, it is your right to speak, ^sirach-32-3

but temper your knowledge and do not interrupt the singing.

4 Where there is entertainment, do not pour out discourse, ^sirach-32-4

and do not display your wisdom at the wrong time.

5 Like a seal of carnelian in a setting of gold: ^sirach-32-5

a concert of music at a banquet of wine.

6 A seal of emerald in a work of gold: ^sirach-32-6

the melody of music with delicious wine.

7 Speak, young man, only when necessary, ^sirach-32-7

when they have asked you more than once.

8 Be brief, say much in few words; ^sirach-32-8

be knowledgeable and yet quiet.

9 When among elders do not be forward, ^sirach-32-9

and with officials do not be too insistent.

10 The lightning that flashes before a hailstorm: ^sirach-32-10

the esteem that shines on modesty.

11 Leave in good time and do not be the last; ^sirach-32-11

go home quickly without delay.

12 There enjoy doing as you wish, ^sirach-32-12

but do not sin through words of pride.

13 Above all, bless your Maker, ^sirach-32-13

who showers his favors upon you.

### The Providence of God

14 Whoever seeks God must accept discipline; ^sirach-32-14

and whoever resorts to him obtains an answer.

15 Whoever seeks the law will master it, ^sirach-32-15

but the hypocrite will be ensnared by it.

16 Whoever fears the LORD will understand what is right, ^sirach-32-16

and out of obscurity he will draw forth a course of action.

17 The lawless turn aside warnings ^sirach-32-17

and distort the law to suit their purpose.

18 The sensible will not neglect direction; ^sirach-32-18

the proud and insolent are deterred by no fear.

19 Do nothing without deliberation; ^sirach-32-19

then once you have acted, have no regrets.

20 Do not go on a way set with snares, ^sirach-32-20

and do not stumble on the same thing twice.

21 Do not trust the road, because of bandits; ^sirach-32-21

22 be careful on your paths. ^sirach-32-22

23 Whatever you do, be on your guard, ^sirach-32-23

for whoever does so keeps the commandments.

24 Whoever keeps the law preserves himself; ^sirach-32-24

and whoever trusts in the LORD shall not be put to shame.

\* (32:14] – [33:4) God is shown to reveal himself through the discipline of his law, a clear and safe plan of life for the pious. Direction and deliberation are aids in following it ([32:14] – [16], [18] – [24]; [33:1], [3] – [4]). Sinners and hypocrites, hating the law or distorting it, fail in wisdom and are devoid of security ([32:15b], [17], [18b]; [33:2]).
