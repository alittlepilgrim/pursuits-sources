# Sirach, Chapter 35

## Chapter 35

1 To keep the law is to make many offerings; ^sirach-35-1

2 whoever observes the commandments sacrifices a peace offering. ^sirach-35-2

3 By works of charity one offers fine flour, ^sirach-35-3

4 and one who gives alms presents a sacrifice of praise. ^sirach-35-4

5 To refrain from evil pleases the Lord, ^sirach-35-5

and to avoid injustice is atonement.

6 Do not appear before the Lord empty-handed, ^sirach-35-6

7 for all that you offer is in fulfillment of the precepts. ^sirach-35-7

8 The offering of the just enriches the altar: ^sirach-35-8

a sweet odor before the Most High.

9 The sacrifice of the just is accepted, ^sirach-35-9

never to be forgotten.

10 With a generous spirit pay homage to the Lord, ^sirach-35-10

and do not spare your freewill gifts.

11 With each contribution show a cheerful countenance, ^sirach-35-11

and pay your tithes in a spirit of joy.

12 Give to the Most High as he has given to you, ^sirach-35-12

generously, according to your means.

13 For he is a God who always repays ^sirach-35-13

and will give back to you sevenfold.

14 But offer no bribes; these he does not accept! ^sirach-35-14

15 Do not trust in sacrifice of the fruits of extortion, ^sirach-35-15

For he is a God of justice,

who shows no partiality.

16 He shows no partiality to the weak ^sirach-35-16

but hears the grievance of the oppressed.

17 He does not forsake the cry of the orphan, ^sirach-35-17

nor the widow when she pours out her complaint.

18 Do not the tears that stream down her cheek ^sirach-35-18

19 cry out against the one that causes them to fall? ^sirach-35-19

20 Those who serve God to please him are accepted; ^sirach-35-20

their petition reaches the clouds.

21 The prayer of the lowly pierces the clouds; ^sirach-35-21

it does not rest till it reaches its goal;

Nor will it withdraw till the Most High responds,

22 judges justly and affirms the right. ^sirach-35-22

God indeed will not delay,

and like a warrior, will not be still

Till he breaks the backs of the merciless

23 and wreaks vengeance upon the nations; ^sirach-35-23

Till he destroys the scepter of the proud,

and cuts off the staff of the wicked;

24 Till he requites everyone according to their deeds, ^sirach-35-24

and repays them according to their thoughts;

25 Till he defends the cause of his people, ^sirach-35-25

and makes them glad by his salvation.

26 Welcome is his mercy in time of distress ^sirach-35-26

as rain clouds in time of drought.

\* (35:1] – [26) Keeping the commandments of the law and avoiding injustice constitute sacrifice pleasing and acceptable to God (vv. [1] – [5]). Offerings also should be made to him, cheerfully and generously; these he repays sevenfold (vv. [6] – [13]). Extortion from widows and orphans is injustice, and God will hear their cries (vv. [14] – [22a]). Punishing the proud and the merciless and coming to the aid of the distressed, he requites everyone according to their deeds (vv. [22b] – [26]).

\* (35:3) Fine flour, together with oil and frankincense, was a prescribed offering to God; cf. [Lv 2:1] – [3].

\* (35:16) Cf. [Lv 19:15]; [Dt 1:17]. The divine impartiality is paradoxical, for it is tilted toward the poor.
