# Sirach, Chapter 34

## Chapter 34

### Trust in the Lord and Not in Dreams

1 Empty and false are the hopes of the senseless, ^sirach-34-1

and dreams give wings to fools.

2 Like one grasping at shadows or chasing the wind, ^sirach-34-2

so anyone who believes in dreams.

3 What is seen in dreams is a reflection, ^sirach-34-3

the likeness of a face looking at itself.

4 How can the unclean produce what is clean? ^sirach-34-4

How can the false produce what is true?

5 Divination, omens, and dreams are unreal; ^sirach-34-5

what you already expect, the mind fantasizes.

6 Unless they are specially sent by the Most High, ^sirach-34-6

do not fix your heart on them.

7 For dreams have led many astray, ^sirach-34-7

and those who put their hope in them have perished.

8 Without such deceptions the Law will be fulfilled, ^sirach-34-8

and in the mouth of the faithful is complete wisdom.

9 A much-traveled person knows many things; ^sirach-34-9

and one with much experience speaks sense.

10 An inexperienced person knows little, ^sirach-34-10

11 whereas with travel one adds to resourcefulness. ^sirach-34-11

12 I have seen much in my travels, ^sirach-34-12

and learned more than I could ever say.

13 Often I was in danger of death, ^sirach-34-13

but by these experiences I was saved.

14 Living is the spirit of those who fear the Lord, ^sirach-34-14

15 for their hope is in their savior. ^sirach-34-15

16 Whoever fear the Lord are afraid of nothing ^sirach-34-16

and are never discouraged, for he is their hope.

17 Happy the soul that fears the Lord! ^sirach-34-17

18 In whom does he trust, and who is his support? ^sirach-34-18

19 The eyes of the Lord are upon those who love him; ^sirach-34-19

he is their mighty shield and strong support,

A shelter from the heat, a shade from the noonday sun,

a guard against stumbling, a help against falling.

20 He lifts up spirits, brings a sparkle to the eyes, ^sirach-34-20

gives health and life and blessing.

### True Worship of God

21 Ill-gotten goods offered in sacrifice are tainted. ^sirach-34-21

22 Presents from the lawless do not win God’s favor. ^sirach-34-22

23 The Most High is not pleased with the gifts of the godless, ^sirach-34-23

nor for their many sacrifices does he forgive their sins.

24 One who slays a son in his father’s presence— ^sirach-34-24

whoever offers sacrifice from the holdings of the poor.

25 The bread of charity is life itself for the needy; ^sirach-34-25

whoever withholds it is a murderer.

26 To take away a neighbor’s living is to commit murder; ^sirach-34-26

27 to deny a laborer wages is to shed blood. ^sirach-34-27

28 If one builds up and another tears down, ^sirach-34-28

what do they gain but trouble?

29 If one prays and another curses, ^sirach-34-29

whose voice will God hear?

30 If one again touches a corpse after bathing, ^sirach-34-30

what does he gain by the purification?

31 So one who fasts for sins, ^sirach-34-31

but goes and commits them again:

Who will hear his prayer,

what is gained by mortification?

\* (34:1] – [20) Confidence placed in dreams, divinations, and omens is false because these are devoid of reality (vv. [1] – [8]). True confidence is founded on knowledge and experience (vv. [9] – [13]), and above all on the fear of the Lord, with its accompanying blessings of divine assistance and protection (vv. [14] – [20]).

\* (34:21] – [31) To be acts of true religion, sacrifice and penance must be accompanied by the proper moral dispositions. To offer to God goods taken from the poor (vv. [21] – [27]), or to practice penance without interior reform, is a mockery, worthless in the sight of God (vv. [28] – [31]). Cf. ([[matthew-15|Mt 15]]:4] – [7]; ([[mark-07|Mk 7]]:9] – [13].
