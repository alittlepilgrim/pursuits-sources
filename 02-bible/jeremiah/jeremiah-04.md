# Jeremiah, Chapter 4

### Chapter 4

1 If you return, Israel—oracle of the L ORD — ^jeremiah-04-1

return to me.

If you put your detestable things out of my sight,

and do not stray,

2 And swear, “As the L ORD lives,” ^jeremiah-04-2

in truth, in judgment, and in justice,

Then the nations shall bless themselves in him

and in him glory.

3 For to the people of Judah and Jerusalem, thus says the L ORD: ^jeremiah-04-3

Till your untilled ground,

and do not sow among thorns.

4 Be circumcised for the L ORD, ^jeremiah-04-4

remove the foreskins of your hearts,

people of Judah and inhabitants of Jerusalem;

Or else my anger will break out like fire,

and burn so that no one can quench it,

because of your evil deeds.

#### The Invasion from the North

5 Proclaim it in Judah, ^jeremiah-04-5

in Jerusalem announce it;

Blow the trumpet throughout the land,

call out, “Fill the ranks!”

Say, “Assemble, let us march

to the fortified cities.”

6 Raise the signal—to Zion! ^jeremiah-04-6

Seek refuge! Don’t stand there!

Disaster I bring from the north,

and great destruction.

7 Up comes the lion from its lair, ^jeremiah-04-7

the destroyer of nations has set out,

has left its place,

To turn your land into a desolation,

your cities into an uninhabited waste.

8 So put on sackcloth, ^jeremiah-04-8

mourn and wail:

“The blazing anger of the L ORD

has not turned away from us.”

9 In that day—oracle of the L ORD — ^jeremiah-04-9

The king will lose heart, and the princes;

the priests will be horrified,

and the prophets stunned.

10 “Ah! Lord G OD,” they will say, ^jeremiah-04-10

“You really did deceive us

When you said: You shall have peace,

while the sword was at our very throats.”

11 At that time it will be said ^jeremiah-04-11

to this people and to Jerusalem,

A scorching wind from the bare heights comes

through the wilderness toward my daughter, the people.

Not to winnow, not to cleanse,

12 a strong wind from there comes at my bidding. ^jeremiah-04-12

Now I too pronounce

sentence upon them.

13 See! like storm clouds he advances, ^jeremiah-04-13

like a whirlwind, his chariots;

Swifter than eagles, his horses:

“Woe to us! we are ruined.”

14 Cleanse your heart of evil, Jerusalem, ^jeremiah-04-14

that you may be saved.

How long will you entertain

wicked schemes?

15 A voice proclaims it from Dan, ^jeremiah-04-15

announces wickedness from Mount Ephraim:

16 “Make this known to the nations, ^jeremiah-04-16

announce it against Jerusalem:

Besiegers are coming from the distant land,

shouting their war cry against the cities of Judah.”

17 Like watchers in the fields they surround her, ^jeremiah-04-17

for she has rebelled against me—oracle of the L ORD.

18 Your conduct, your deeds, have done this to you; ^jeremiah-04-18

how bitter is this evil of yours,

how it reaches to your very heart!

19 My body! my body! how I writhe! ^jeremiah-04-19

The walls of my heart!

My heart beats wildly,

I cannot be still;

For I myself have heard the blast of the horn,

the battle cry.

20 Ruin upon ruin is reported; ^jeremiah-04-20

the whole land is laid waste.

In an instant my tents are ravaged;

in a flash, my shelters.

21 How long must I see the signal, ^jeremiah-04-21

hear the blast of the horn!

22 My people are fools, ^jeremiah-04-22

they do not know me;

They are senseless children,

without understanding;

They are wise at evil,

but they do not know how to do good.

23 I looked at the earth—it was waste and void; ^jeremiah-04-23

at the heavens—their light had gone out!

24 I looked at the mountains—they were quaking! ^jeremiah-04-24

All the hills were crumbling!

25 I looked—there was no one; ^jeremiah-04-25

even the birds of the air had flown away!

26 I looked—the garden land was a wilderness, ^jeremiah-04-26

with all its cities destroyed

before the L ORD, before his blazing anger.

27 For thus says the L ORD: ^jeremiah-04-27

The whole earth shall be waste,

but I will not wholly destroy it.

28 Because of this the earth shall mourn, ^jeremiah-04-28

the heavens above shall darken;

I have spoken, I will not change my mind,

I have decided, I will not turn back.

29 At the shout of rider and archer ^jeremiah-04-29

each city takes to flight;

They shrink into the thickets,

they scale the rocks:

All the cities are abandoned,

no one lives in them.

30 You now who are doomed, what are you doing ^jeremiah-04-30

dressing in purple,

bedecking yourself with gold,

Enlarging your eyes with kohl?

You beautify yourself in vain!

Your lovers reject you,

they seek your life.

31 Yes, I hear the cry, like that of a woman in labor, ^jeremiah-04-31

like the anguish of a mother bearing her first child—

The cry of daughter Zion gasping,

as she stretches out her hands:

“Ah, woe is me! I sink exhausted

before my killers!”

\* (4:2) As the L ORD lives: this oath, made sincerely, implies Israel’s return and loyal adherence to God. Thus the ancient promises are fulfilled; cf. [[genesis-12|Gen 12]]:3; [18:18]; [22:18]; [26:4]; [Ps 72:17].

\* (4:4) The external rite of circumcision accomplishes nothing unless it is accompanied by the removal of blindness and obstinacy of heart. Jeremiah’s view is reflected in ([[romans-02|Rom 2]]:25], [29]; ([[1-corinthians-07|1 Cor 7]]:19]; [Gal 5:6]; [6:13], [15].

\* (4:10) You really did deceive: Jeremiah complains that the Lord misled the people by fostering their complacency, leaving them unprepared and unrepentant as judgment approaches.

\* (4:11) My daughter, the people: the covenant people personified as a young woman. [Ezekiel 16] presents Israel and Judah as female infants whom the Lord adopts and then abandons and punishes because they desire other lords.

\* (4:19] – [21) Probably the prophet’s own anguish at the coming destruction of Judah.
