# Jeremiah, Chapter 45

## Chapter 45

**A Message to Baruch.**

1 The word that Jeremiah the prophet spoke to Baruch, son of Neriah, when he wrote on a scroll words from Jeremiah’s own mouth in the fourth year of Jehoiakim, son of Josiah, king of Judah: ^jeremiah-45-1

2 Thus says the LORD, God of Israel, to you, Baruch. ^jeremiah-45-2

3 You said, “Woe is me! the LORD has added grief to my pain. I have worn myself out with groaning; rest eludes me.” ^jeremiah-45-3

4 You must say this to him. Thus says the LORD: What I have built, I am tearing down; what I have planted, I am uprooting: all this land. ^jeremiah-45-4

5 And you, do you seek great things for yourself? Do not seek them! I am bringing evil on all flesh—oracle of the LORD —but I will grant you your life as spoils of war, wherever you may go. ^jeremiah-45-5

\* (45:1] – [5) At the conclusion of his narrative, Baruch appends a message Jeremiah had given him when he first wrote down Jeremiah’s words (cf. [36:4]). The future revealed by the prophet overwhelmed Baruch; now he learns his own safety is assured, even though the Lord will destroy Judah.

a. (45:1) [Jer 36:4], [18], [32].

b. (45:3) [Lam 1:3]; [5:5].

c. (45:4) [Jer 18:7]; [Is 5:5] – [6].

d. (45:5) [Jer 25:26] – [29].
