# Psalm 89

## A Lament over God’s Promise to David

1 A *maskil* of Ethan the Ezrahite. ^psalm-89-1

### A

### I

2 I will sing of your mercy forever, LORD ^psalm-89-2

proclaim your faithfulness through all ages.

3 For I said, “My mercy is established forever; ^psalm-89-3

my faithfulness will stand as long as the heavens.

4 I have made a covenant with my chosen one; ^psalm-89-4

I have sworn to David my servant:

5 I will make your dynasty stand forever ^psalm-89-5

and establish your throne through all ages.”

*Selah*

### II

6 The heavens praise your marvels, LORD, ^psalm-89-6

your loyalty in the assembly of the holy ones.

7 Who in the skies ranks with the LORD? ^psalm-89-7

Who is like the LORD among the sons of the gods?

8 A God dreaded in the council of the holy ones, ^psalm-89-8

greater and more awesome than all those around him!

9 LORD, God of hosts, who is like you? ^psalm-89-9

Mighty LORD, your faithfulness surrounds you.

10 You rule the raging sea; ^psalm-89-10

you still its swelling waves.

11 You crush Rahab with a mortal blow; ^psalm-89-11

with your strong arm you scatter your foes.

12 Yours are the heavens, yours the earth; ^psalm-89-12

you founded the world and everything in it.

13 Zaphon and Amanus you created; ^psalm-89-13

Tabor and Hermon rejoice in your name.

14 You have a mighty arm. ^psalm-89-14

Your hand is strong; your right hand is ever exalted.

15 Justice and judgment are the foundation of your throne; ^psalm-89-15

mercy and faithfulness march before you.

16 Blessed the people who know the war cry, ^psalm-89-16

who walk in the radiance of your face, LORD.

17 In your name they sing joyfully all the day; ^psalm-89-17

they rejoice in your righteousness.

18 You are their majestic strength; ^psalm-89-18

by your favor our horn is exalted.

19 Truly the LORD is our shield, ^psalm-89-19

the Holy One of Israel, our king!

### III

20 Then you spoke in vision; ^psalm-89-20

to your faithful ones you said:

“I have set a leader over the warriors;

I have raised up a chosen one from the people.

21 I have chosen David, my servant; ^psalm-89-21

with my holy oil I have anointed him.

22 My hand will be with him; ^psalm-89-22

my arm will make him strong.

23 No enemy shall outwit him, ^psalm-89-23

nor shall the wicked defeat him.

24 I will crush his foes before him, ^psalm-89-24

strike down those who hate him.

25 My faithfulness and mercy will be with him; ^psalm-89-25

through my name his horn will be exalted.

26 I will set his hand upon the sea, ^psalm-89-26

his right hand upon the rivers.

27 He shall cry to me, ‘You are my father, ^psalm-89-27

my God, the Rock of my salvation!’

28 I myself make him the firstborn, ^psalm-89-28

Most High over the kings of the earth.

29 Forever I will maintain my mercy for him; ^psalm-89-29

my covenant with him stands firm.

30 I will establish his dynasty forever, ^psalm-89-30

his throne as the days of the heavens.

31 If his descendants forsake my teaching, ^psalm-89-31

do not follow my decrees,

32 If they fail to observe my statutes, ^psalm-89-32

do not keep my commandments,

33 I will punish their crime with a rod ^psalm-89-33

and their guilt with blows.

34 But I will not take my mercy from him, ^psalm-89-34

nor will I betray my bond of faithfulness.

35 I will not violate my covenant; ^psalm-89-35

the promise of my lips I will not alter.

36 By my holiness I swore once for all: ^psalm-89-36

I will never be false to David.

37 His dynasty will continue forever, ^psalm-89-37

his throne, like the sun before me.

38 Like the moon it will stand eternal, ^psalm-89-38

forever firm like the sky!”

*Selah*

### B

### IV

39 But now you have rejected and spurned, ^psalm-89-39

been enraged at your anointed.

40 You renounced the covenant with your servant, ^psalm-89-40

defiled his crown in the dust.

41 You broke down all city walls, ^psalm-89-41

left his strongholds in ruins.

42 All who pass through seize plunder; ^psalm-89-42

his neighbors deride him.

43 You have exalted the right hand of his foes, ^psalm-89-43

have gladdened all his enemies.

44 You turned back his sharp sword, ^psalm-89-44

did not support him in battle.

45 You brought to an end his splendor, ^psalm-89-45

hurled his throne to the ground.

46 You cut short the days of his youth, ^psalm-89-46

covered him with shame.

*Selah*

V

47 How long, LORD? Will you hide forever? ^psalm-89-47

Must your wrath smolder like fire?

48 Remember how brief life is, ^psalm-89-48

how frail the sons of man you have created!

49 What is man, that he should live and not see death? ^psalm-89-49

Who can deliver his soul from the power of Sheol?

*Selah*

VI

50 Where are your former mercies, Lord, ^psalm-89-50

that you swore to David in your faithfulness?

51 Remember, Lord, the insults to your servants, ^psalm-89-51

how I have borne in my bosom the slander of the nations.

52 Your enemies, LORD, insult; ^psalm-89-52

they insult each step of your anointed.

53 Blessed be the LORD forever! Amen and amen! ^psalm-89-53

\* (Psalm 89) The community laments the defeat of the Davidic king, to whom God promised kingship as enduring as the heavens ([Ps 89:2] – [5]). The Psalm narrates how God became king of the divine beings ([Ps 89:6] – [9]) and how the Davidic king became king of earthly kings ([Ps 89:20] – [38]). Since the defeat of the king calls into question God’s promise, the community ardently prays God to be faithful to the original promise to David ([Ps 89:39] – [52]).

\* (89:3] – [5) David’s dynasty is to be as long-lasting as the heavens, a statement reinforced by using the same verbs (establish, stand) both of the divine love and loyalty and of the Davidic dynasty and throne, cf. [Ps 89:29] – [30].

\* (89:7) The sons of the gods: “the holy ones” and “courtiers” of [Ps 89:6], [8]. These heavenly spirits are members of God’s court.

\* (89:11) Rahab: a mythological sea monster whose name is used in the Bible mainly as a personification of primeval chaos, cf. [Jb 9:13]; [26:12]; [Ps 74:13] – [14]; [Is 51:9].

\* (89:13) Zaphon and Amanus: two sacred mountains in northern Syria which came to designate the directions of north and south. Tabor: a high hill in the valley of Jezreel in northern Israel. Hermon: a mountain in Lebanon, forming the southern spur of the Anti-Lebanon range.

\* (89:18], [25) Horn: a concrete noun for an abstract quality; horn is a symbol of strength.

\* (89:26) The seaâ€¦the rivers: geographically the limits of the Davidic empire (the Mediterranean and the Euphrates); mythologically, the traditional forces of chaos. See note on [Ps 89:11].

\* (89:28) Most High: a divine title, which is here extended to David as God’s own king, cf. ([[psalm-02|Ps 2]]:7] – [9]; [Is 9:5]. As God rules over the members of the heavenly council ([Ps 89:6] – [9]), so David, God’s surrogate, rules over earthly kings.

\* (89:37] – [38) Like the sun before meâ€¦like the sky: as enduring as the heavenly lights, cf. [Ps 89:2] – [5] and [Ps 72:5], [17].

\* (89:53) The doxology at the end of the third book of the Psalms; it is not part of [Ps 89].
