### Psalm 34

### Thanksgiving to God Who Delivers the Just

1 Of David, when he feigned madness before Abimelech, who drove him out and he went away. ^psalm-34-1

#### I

2 I will bless the L ORD at all times; ^psalm-34-2

his praise shall be always in my mouth.

3 My soul will glory in the L ORD; ^psalm-34-3

let the poor hear and be glad.

4 Magnify the L ORD with me; ^psalm-34-4

and let us exalt his name together.

#### Ii

5 I sought the L ORD, and he answered me, ^psalm-34-5

delivered me from all my fears.

6 Look to him and be radiant, ^psalm-34-6

and your faces may not blush for shame.

7 This poor one cried out and the L ORD heard, ^psalm-34-7

and from all his distress he saved him.

8 The angel of the L ORD encamps ^psalm-34-8

around those who fear him, and he saves them.

9 Taste and see that the L ORD is good; ^psalm-34-9

blessed is the stalwart one who takes refuge in him.

10 Fear the L ORD, you his holy ones; ^psalm-34-10

nothing is lacking to those who fear him.

11 The rich grow poor and go hungry, ^psalm-34-11

but those who seek the L ORD lack no good thing.

#### Iii

12 Come, children, listen to me; ^psalm-34-12

I will teach you fear of the L ORD.

13 Who is the man who delights in life, ^psalm-34-13

who loves to see the good days?

14 Keep your tongue from evil, ^psalm-34-14

your lips from speaking lies.

15 Turn from evil and do good; ^psalm-34-15

seek peace and pursue it.

16 The eyes of the L ORD are directed toward the righteous ^psalm-34-16

and his ears toward their cry.

17 The L ORD’s face is against evildoers ^psalm-34-17

to wipe out their memory from the earth.

18 The righteous cry out, the L ORD hears ^psalm-34-18

and he rescues them from all their afflictions.

19 The L ORD is close to the brokenhearted, ^psalm-34-19

saves those whose spirit is crushed.

20 Many are the troubles of the righteous, ^psalm-34-20

but the L ORD delivers him from them all.

21 He watches over all his bones; ^psalm-34-21

not one of them shall be broken.

22 Evil will slay the wicked; ^psalm-34-22

those who hate the righteous are condemned.

23 The L ORD is the redeemer of the souls of his servants; ^psalm-34-23

and none are condemned who take refuge in him.

\* (Psalm 34) A thanksgiving in acrostic form, each line beginning with a successive letter of the Hebrew alphabet. In this Psalm one letter is missing and two are in reverse order. The psalmist, fresh from the experience of being rescued ([Ps 34:5], [7]), can teach the “poor,” those who are defenseless, to trust in God alone ([Ps 34:4], [12]). God will make them powerful ([Ps 34:5] – [11]) and give them protection ([Ps 34:12] – [22]).

\* (34:1) Abimelech: a scribal error for Achish. In [1 Sm 21:13] – [16], David feigned madness before Achish, not Abimelech.

\* (34:12) Children: the customary term for students in wisdom literature.

a. (34:2) [Ps 145:2].

b. (34:8) ([[exodus-14|Ex 14]]:19].

c. (34:9) ([[psalm-02|Ps 2]]:11].

d. (34:10) [Prv 3:7].

e. (34:12) [Prv 1:8]; [4:1].

f. (34:13] – [17) [1 Pt 3:10] – [12].

g. (34:15) [Ps 37:27].

h. (34:16) [Ps 33:18].

i. (34:21) [Jn 19:36].
