### Psalm 11

### Confidence in the Presence of God

1 For the leader. Of David. ^psalm-11-1

#### I

In the L ORD I take refuge;

how can you say to me,

“Flee like a bird to the mountains!

2 See how the wicked string their bows, ^psalm-11-2

fit their arrows to the string

to shoot from the shadows at the upright of heart.

3 If foundations are destroyed, ^psalm-11-3

what can the just one do?”

#### Ii

4 The L ORD is in his holy temple; ^psalm-11-4

the L ORD’s throne is in heaven.

God’s eyes keep careful watch;

they test the children of Adam.

5 The L ORD tests the righteous and the wicked, ^psalm-11-5

hates those who love violence,

6 And rains upon the wicked ^psalm-11-6

fiery coals and brimstone,

a scorching wind their allotted cup.

7 The L ORD is just and loves just deeds; ^psalm-11-7

the upright will see his face.

\* (Psalm 11) A song of trust. Though friends counsel flight to the mountain country (a traditional hideout) to escape trouble (([[psalm-11|Ps 11]]:1] – [3]), the innocent psalmist reaffirms confidence in God, who protects those who seek asylum in the Temple (([[psalm-11|Ps 11]]:4] – [7]).

\* (11:3) Foundations: usually understood of public order, cf. [Ps 82:5].

\* (11:6) Their allotted cup: the cup that God gives people to drink is a common figure for their destiny, cf. ([[psalm-16|Ps 16]]:5]; [75:9]; ([[matthew-20|Mt 20]]:22]; [26:39]; [Rv 14:10].

a. (11:1) [Ps 55:7]; [91:4].

b. (11:2) ([[psalm-07|Ps 7]]:13]; [37:14]; [57:5]; [64:4].

c. (11:4) ([[psalm-14|Ps 14]]:2]; [102:20]; [Hb 2:20]; [Dt 26:15]; [Is 66:1]; ([[matthew-05|Mt 5]]:34].

d. (11:6) [Ps 120:4]; [140:11]; [Prv 16:27]; [Ez 38:22]; ([[revelation-08|Rv 8]]:5]; [20:10].
