# Psalm 50

## The Acceptable Sacrifice

1 A psalm of Asaph. ^psalm-50-1

### I

The God of gods, the LORD,

has spoken and summoned the earth

from the rising of the sun to its setting.

2 From Zion, the perfection of beauty, ^psalm-50-2

God shines forth.

3 Our God comes and will not be silent! ^psalm-50-3

Devouring fire precedes him,

it rages strongly around him.

4 He calls to the heavens above ^psalm-50-4

and to the earth to judge his people:

5 “Gather my loyal ones to me, ^psalm-50-5

those who made a covenant with me by sacrifice.”

6 The heavens proclaim his righteousness, ^psalm-50-6

for God himself is the judge.

*Selah*

### II

7 “Listen, my people, I will speak; ^psalm-50-7

Israel, I will testify against you;

God, your God, am I.

8 Not for your sacrifices do I rebuke you, ^psalm-50-8

your burnt offerings are always before me.

9 I will not take a bullock from your house, ^psalm-50-9

or he-goats from your folds.

10 For every animal of the forest is mine, ^psalm-50-10

beasts by the thousands on my mountains.

11 I know every bird in the heights; ^psalm-50-11

whatever moves in the wild is mine.

12 Were I hungry, I would not tell you, ^psalm-50-12

for mine is the world and all that fills it.

13 Do I eat the flesh of bulls ^psalm-50-13

or drink the blood of he-goats?

14 Offer praise as your sacrifice to God; ^psalm-50-14

fulfill your vows to the Most High.

15 Then call on me on the day of distress; ^psalm-50-15

I will rescue you, and you shall honor me.”

### III

16 But to the wicked God says: ^psalm-50-16

“Why do you recite my commandments

and profess my covenant with your mouth?

17 You hate discipline; ^psalm-50-17

you cast my words behind you!

18 If you see a thief, you run with him; ^psalm-50-18

with adulterers you throw in your lot.

19 You give your mouth free rein for evil; ^psalm-50-19

you yoke your tongue to deceit.

20 You sit and speak against your brother, ^psalm-50-20

slandering your mother’s son.

21 When you do these things should I be silent? ^psalm-50-21

Do you think that I am like you?

I accuse you, I lay out the matter before your eyes.

### IV

22 “Now understand this, you who forget God, ^psalm-50-22

lest I start ripping apart and there be no rescuer.

23 Those who offer praise as a sacrifice honor me; ^psalm-50-23

I will let him whose way is steadfast

look upon the salvation of God.”

\* (Psalm 50) A covenant lawsuit stating that the sacrifice God really wants is the sacrifice of praise accompanied by genuine obedience (cf. [Mi 6:1] – [8]). It begins with a theophany and the summoning of the court ([Ps 50:1] – [6]). Then in direct address God explains what is required of the faithful ([Ps 50:7] – [15]), rebukes the hypocritical worshiper ([Ps 50:16] – [21]), and concludes with a threat and a promise ([Ps 50:22] – [23]; cf. [Is 1:19] – [20]).
