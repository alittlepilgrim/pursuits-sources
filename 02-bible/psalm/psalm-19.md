# Psalm 19

## God’s Glory in the Heavens and in the Law

1 For the leader. A psalm of David. ^psalm-19-1

### I

2 The heavens declare the glory of God; ^psalm-19-2

the firmament proclaims the works of his hands.

3 Day unto day pours forth speech; ^psalm-19-3

night unto night whispers knowledge.

4 There is no speech, no words; ^psalm-19-4

their voice is not heard;

5 A report goes forth through all the earth, ^psalm-19-5

their messages, to the ends of the world.

He has pitched in them a tent for the sun;

6 it comes forth like a bridegroom from his canopy, ^psalm-19-6

and like a hero joyfully runs its course.

7 From one end of the heavens it comes forth; ^psalm-19-7

its course runs through to the other;

nothing escapes its heat.

### II

8 The law of the LORD is perfect, ^psalm-19-8

refreshing the soul.

The decree of the LORD is trustworthy,

giving wisdom to the simple.

9 The precepts of the LORD are right, ^psalm-19-9

rejoicing the heart.

The command of the LORD is clear,

enlightening the eye.

10 The fear of the LORD is pure, ^psalm-19-10

enduring forever.

The statutes of the LORD are true,

all of them just;

11 More desirable than gold, ^psalm-19-11

than a hoard of purest gold,

Sweeter also than honey

or drippings from the comb.

12 By them your servant is warned; ^psalm-19-12

obeying them brings much reward.

### III

13 Who can detect trespasses? ^psalm-19-13

Cleanse me from my inadvertent sins.

14 Also from arrogant ones restrain your servant; ^psalm-19-14

let them never control me.

Then shall I be blameless,

innocent of grave sin.

15 Let the words of my mouth be acceptable, ^psalm-19-15

the thoughts of my heart before you,

LORD, my rock and my redeemer.

\* (Psalm 19) The heavenly elements of the world, now beautifully arranged, bespeak the power and wisdom of their creator (([[psalm-19|Ps 19]]:2] – [7]). The creator’s wisdom is available to human beings in the law (([[psalm-19|Ps 19]]:8] – [11]), toward which the psalmist prays to be open (([[psalm-19|Ps 19]]:12] – [14]). The themes of light and speech unify the poem.

\* (19:4) No speech, no words: the regular functioning of the heavens and the alternation of day and night inform human beings without words of the creator’s power and wisdom.

\* (19:5) The sun: in other religious literature the sun is a judge and lawgiver since it sees all in its daily course; ([[psalm-19|Ps 19]]:5b] – [7] form a transition to the law in ([[psalm-19|Ps 19]]:8] – [11]. The six synonyms for God’s revelation (([[psalm-19|Ps 19]]:8] – [11]) are applied to the sun in comparable literature.

\* (19:12) Warned: the Hebrew verb means both to shine and to warn, cf. [Dn 12:3].
