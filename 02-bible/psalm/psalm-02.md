# Psalm 2

## A Psalm for a Royal Coronation

1 Why do the nations protest ^psalm-02-1

and the peoples conspire in vain?

2 Kings on earth rise up ^psalm-02-2

and princes plot together

against the LORD and against his anointed one:

3 “Let us break their shackles ^psalm-02-3

and cast off their chains from us!”

4 The one enthroned in heaven laughs; ^psalm-02-4

the Lord derides them,

5 Then he speaks to them in his anger, ^psalm-02-5

in his wrath he terrifies them:

6 “I myself have installed my king ^psalm-02-6

on Zion, my holy mountain.”

7 I will proclaim the decree of the LORD, ^psalm-02-7

he said to me, “You are my son;

today I have begotten you.

8 Ask it of me, ^psalm-02-8

and I will give you the nations as your inheritance,

and, as your possession, the ends of the earth.

9 With an iron rod you will shepherd them, ^psalm-02-9

like a potter’s vessel you will shatter them.”

10 And now, kings, give heed; ^psalm-02-10

take warning, judges on earth.

11 Serve the LORD with fear; ^psalm-02-11

exult with trembling,

Accept correction

lest he become angry and you perish along the way

when his anger suddenly blazes up.

Blessed are all who take refuge in him!

\* (Psalm 2) A royal Psalm. To rebellious kings (([[psalm-02|Ps 2]]:1] – [3]) God responds vigorously (([[psalm-02|Ps 2]]:4] – [6]). A speaker proclaims the divine decree (in the legal adoption language of the day), making the Israelite king the earthly representative of God (([[psalm-02|Ps 2]]:7] – [9]) and warning kings to obey (([[psalm-02|Ps 2]]:10] – [11]). The Psalm has a messianic meaning for the Church; the New Testament understands it of Christ ([.[[acts-of-the-apostles-04|Acts 4]]:25] – [27]; [13:33]; [Heb 1:5]).

\* (2:2) Anointed: in Hebrew *mashiah*, “anointed”; in Greek *christos*, whence English Messiah and Christ. In Israel kings ([Jgs 9:8]; [1 Sm 9:16]; [16:12] – [13]) and high priests ([Lv 8:12]; [Nm 3:3]) received the power of their office through anointing.
