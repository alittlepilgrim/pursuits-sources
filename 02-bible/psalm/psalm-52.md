# Psalm 52

## The Deceitful Tongue

1 For the leader. A *maskil* of David, ^psalm-52-1

2 when Doeg the Edomite entered and reported to Saul, saying to him: “David has entered the house of Ahimelech.” ^psalm-52-2

### I

3 Why do you glory in what is evil, you who are mighty by the mercy of God? ^psalm-52-3

All day long

4 you are thinking up intrigues; ^psalm-52-4

your tongue is like a sharpened razor,

you worker of deceit.

5 You love evil more than good, ^psalm-52-5

lying rather than saying what is right.

*Selah*

6 You love all the words that create confusion, ^psalm-52-6

you deceitful tongue.

### II

7 God too will strike you down forever, ^psalm-52-7

he will lay hold of you and pluck you from your tent,

uproot you from the land of the living.

*Selah*

8 The righteous will see and they will fear; ^psalm-52-8

but they will laugh at him:

9 “Behold the man! He did not take God as his refuge, ^psalm-52-9

but he trusted in the abundance of his wealth,

and grew powerful through his wickedness.”

### III

10 But I, like an olive tree flourishing in the house of God, ^psalm-52-10

I trust in God’s mercy forever and ever.

11 I will thank you forever ^psalm-52-11

for what you have done.

I will put my hope in your name—for it is good,

—in the presence of those devoted to you.

\* (Psalm 52) A condemnation of the powerful and arrogant ([Ps 52:3] – [6]), who bring down upon themselves God’s judgment ([Ps 52:7]). The just, those who trust in God alone, are gladdened and strengthened by the downfall of their traditional enemies ([Ps 52:8] – [9]).

\* (52:10) Like an olive tree: the righteous will flourish in the house of God like a well-watered olive tree, cf. [Ps 92:14]; [128:3].
