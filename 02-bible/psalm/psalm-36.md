# Psalm 36

## Human Wickedness and Divine Providence

1 For the leader. Of David, the servant of the LORD. ^psalm-36-1

### I

2 Sin directs the heart of the wicked man; ^psalm-36-2

his eyes are closed to the fear of God.

3 For he lives with the delusion: ^psalm-36-3

his guilt will not be known and hated.

4 Empty and false are the words of his mouth; ^psalm-36-4

he has ceased to be wise and do good.

5 On his bed he hatches plots; ^psalm-36-5

he sets out on a wicked way;

he does not reject evil.

### II

6 LORD, your mercy reaches to heaven; ^psalm-36-6

your fidelity, to the clouds.

7 Your justice is like the highest mountains; ^psalm-36-7

your judgments, like the mighty deep;

human being and beast you sustain, LORD.

8 How precious is your mercy, O God! ^psalm-36-8

The children of Adam take refuge in the shadow of your wings.

9 They feast on the rich food of your house; ^psalm-36-9

from your delightful stream you give them drink.

10 For with you is the fountain of life, ^psalm-36-10

and in your light we see light.

11 Show mercy on those who know you, ^psalm-36-11

your just defense to the upright of heart.

12 Do not let the foot of the proud overtake me, ^psalm-36-12

nor the hand of the wicked disturb me.

13 There make the evildoers fall; ^psalm-36-13

thrust them down, unable to rise.

\* (Psalm 36) A Psalm with elements of wisdom ([Ps 36:2] – [5]), the hymn ([Ps 36:6] – [10]), and the lament ([Ps 36:11] – [13]). The rule of sin over the wicked ([Ps 36:2] – [5]) is contrasted with the rule of divine love and mercy over God’s friends ([Ps 36:6] – [10]). The Psalm ends with a prayer that God’s guidance never cease ([Ps 36:11] – [12]).

\* (36:3) Hated: punished by God.

\* (36:6] – [7) God actively controls the entire world.

\* (36:8) The shadow of your wings: metaphor for divine protection. It probably refers to the winged cherubim in the holy of holies in the Temple, cf. [1 Kgs 6:23] – [28], [32]; [2 Chr 3:10] – [13]; ([[ezekiel-01|Ez 1]]:4] – [9].
