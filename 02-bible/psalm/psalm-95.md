# Psalm 95

## A Call to Praise and Obedience

### I

1 Come, let us sing joyfully to the LORD; ^psalm-95-1

cry out to the rock of our salvation.

2 Let us come before him with a song of praise, ^psalm-95-2

joyfully sing out our psalms.

3 For the LORD is the great God, ^psalm-95-3

the great king over all gods,

4 Whose hand holds the depths of the earth; ^psalm-95-4

who owns the tops of the mountains.

5 The sea and dry land belong to God, ^psalm-95-5

who made them, formed them by hand.

### II

6 Enter, let us bow down in worship; ^psalm-95-6

let us kneel before the LORD who made us.

7 For he is our God, ^psalm-95-7

we are the people he shepherds,

the sheep in his hands.

### III

Oh, that today you would hear his voice:

8 Do not harden your hearts as at Meribah, ^psalm-95-8

as on the day of Massah in the desert.

9 There your ancestors tested me; ^psalm-95-9

they tried me though they had seen my works.

10 Forty years I loathed that generation; ^psalm-95-10

I said: “This people’s heart goes astray;

they do not know my ways.”

11 Therefore I swore in my anger: ^psalm-95-11

“They shall never enter my rest.”

\* (Psalm 95) Twice the Psalm calls the people to praise and worship God ([Ps 95:1] – [2], [6]), the king of all creatures ([Ps 95:3] – [5]) and shepherd of the flock ([Ps 95:7a], [7b]). The last strophe warns the people to be more faithful than were their ancestors in the journey to the promised land ([Ps 95:7c] – [11]). This invitation to praise God regularly opens the Church’s official prayer, the Liturgy of the Hours.

\* (95:8) Meribah: lit., “contention”; the place where the Israelites quarreled with God. Massah: “testing,” the place where they put God to the trial, cf. ([[exodus-17|Ex 17]]:7]; [Nm 20:13].

\* (95:11) My rest: the promised land as in [Dt 12:9]. [Heb 4] applies the verse to the eternal rest of heaven.
