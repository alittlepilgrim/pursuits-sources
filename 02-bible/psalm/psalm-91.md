# Psalm 91

## Security Under God’s Protection

### I

1 You who dwell in the shelter of the Most High, ^psalm-91-1

who abide in the shade of the Almighty,

2 Say to the LORD, “My refuge and fortress, ^psalm-91-2

my God in whom I trust.”

3 He will rescue you from the fowler’s snare, ^psalm-91-3

from the destroying plague,

4 He will shelter you with his pinions, ^psalm-91-4

and under his wings you may take refuge;

his faithfulness is a protecting shield.

5 You shall not fear the terror of the night ^psalm-91-5

nor the arrow that flies by day,

6 Nor the pestilence that roams in darkness, ^psalm-91-6

nor the plague that ravages at noon.

7 Though a thousand fall at your side, ^psalm-91-7

ten thousand at your right hand,

near you it shall not come.

8 You need simply watch; ^psalm-91-8

the punishment of the wicked you will see.

9 Because you have the LORD for your refuge ^psalm-91-9

and have made the Most High your stronghold,

10 No evil shall befall you, ^psalm-91-10

no affliction come near your tent.

11 For he commands his angels with regard to you, ^psalm-91-11

to guard you wherever you go.

12 With their hands they shall support you, ^psalm-91-12

lest you strike your foot against a stone.

13 You can tread upon the asp and the viper, ^psalm-91-13

trample the lion and the dragon.

### II

14 Because he clings to me I will deliver him; ^psalm-91-14

because he knows my name I will set him on high.

15 He will call upon me and I will answer; ^psalm-91-15

I will be with him in distress;

I will deliver him and give him honor.

16 With length of days I will satisfy him, ^psalm-91-16

and fill him with my saving power.

\* (Psalm 91) A prayer of someone who has taken refuge in the Lord, possibly within the Temple ([Ps 91:1] – [2]). The psalmist is confident that God’s presence will protect the people in every dangerous situation ([Ps 91:3] – [13]). The final verses are an oracle of salvation promising salvation to those who trust in God ([Ps 91:14] – [16]).

\* (91:1) The shelter of the Most High: basically “hiding place” but in the Psalms a designation for the protected Temple precincts, cf. ([[psalm-27|Ps 27]]:5]; [31:21]; [61:5]. The shade of the Almighty: lit., “the shadow of the wings of the Almighty,” cf. ([[psalm-17|Ps 17]]:8]; [36:8]; [57:2]; [63:8]. [Ps 91:4] makes clear that the shadow is an image of the safety afforded by the outstretched wings of the cherubim in the holy of holies.

\* (91:11] – [12) The words are cited in ([[luke-04| [[luke-04|Lk 4]]:]]:10] – [11]; ([[matthew-04|Mt 4]]:6], as Satan tempts Jesus in the desert.
