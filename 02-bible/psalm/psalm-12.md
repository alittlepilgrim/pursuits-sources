### Psalm 12

### Prayer Against Evil Tongues

1 For the leader; “upon the eighth.” A psalm of David. ^psalm-12-1

#### I

2 Help, L ORD, for no one loyal remains; ^psalm-12-2

the faithful have vanished from the children of men.

3 They tell lies to one another, ^psalm-12-3

speak with deceiving lips and a double heart.

#### Ii

4 May the L ORD cut off all deceiving lips, ^psalm-12-4

and every boastful tongue,

5 Those who say, “By our tongues we prevail; ^psalm-12-5

when our lips speak, who can lord it over us?”

#### Iii

6 “Because they rob the weak, and the needy groan, ^psalm-12-6

I will now arise,” says the L ORD;

“I will grant safety to whoever longs for it.”

#### Iv

7 The promises of the L ORD are sure, ^psalm-12-7

silver refined in a crucible,

silver purified seven times.

8 You, O L ORD, protect us always; ^psalm-12-8

preserve us from this generation.

9 On every side the wicked roam; ^psalm-12-9

the shameless are extolled by the children of men.

\* (Psalm 12) A lament. The psalmist, thrown into a world where lying and violent people persecute the just (([[psalm-12|Ps 12]]:2] – [3]), prays that the wicked be punished (([[psalm-12|Ps 12]]:4] – [5]). The prayer is not simply for vengeance but arises from a desire to see God’s justice appear on earth. ([[psalm-12|Ps 12]]:6] preserves the word of assurance spoken by the priest to the lamenter; it is not usually transmitted in such Psalms. In ([[psalm-12|Ps 12]]:7] – [8] the psalmist affirms the intention to live by the word of assurance.

\* (12:7) A crucible: lit., “in a crucible in the ground.” The crucible was placed in the ground for support.

c. (12:5) [Sir 5:3].

d. (12:6) [Is 33:10].
