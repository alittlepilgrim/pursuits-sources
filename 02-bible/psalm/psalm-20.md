### Psalm 20

### Prayer for the King in Time of War

1 For the leader. A psalm of David. ^psalm-20-1

#### I

2 The L ORD answer you in time of distress; ^psalm-20-2

the name of the God of Jacob defend you!

3 May he send you help from the sanctuary, ^psalm-20-3

from Zion be your support.

4 May he remember your every offering, ^psalm-20-4

graciously accept your burnt offering,

*Selah*

5 Grant what is in your heart, ^psalm-20-5

fulfill your every plan.

6 May we shout for joy at your victory, ^psalm-20-6

raise the banners in the name of our God.

The L ORD grant your every petition!

#### Ii

7 Now I know the L ORD gives victory ^psalm-20-7

to his anointed.

He will answer him from the holy heavens

with a strong arm that brings victory.

8 Some rely on chariots, others on horses, ^psalm-20-8

but we on the name of the L ORD our God.

9 They collapse and fall, ^psalm-20-9

but we stand strong and firm.

10 L ORD, grant victory to the king; ^psalm-20-10

answer when we call upon you.

\* (Psalm 20) The people pray for the king before battle. The people ask for divine help (([[psalm-20|Ps 20]]:2] – [6]) and express confidence that such help will be given (([[psalm-20|Ps 20]]:7] – [10]). A solemn assurance of divine help may well have been given between the two sections in the liturgy, something like the promises of ([[psalm-12|Ps 12]]:6]; [21:9] – [13]. The final verse (([[psalm-20|Ps 20]]:10]) echoes the opening verse.

\* (20:4) Remember: God’s remembering implies readiness to act, cf. [Gn 8:1]; ([[exodus-02|Ex 2]]:24].

\* (20:6) Victory: the Hebrew root is often translated “salvation,” “to save,” but in military contexts it can have the specific meaning of “victory.”

a. (20:3) [Ps 128:5]; [134:3].

b. (20:7) ([[psalm-18|Ps 18]]:51]; [144:10]; [1 Sm 2:10].

c. (20:8) [Ps 147:10] – [11]; [2 Chr 14:10]; [Prv 21:31]; [1 Sm 17:45]; [Is 31:1]; [36:9].

d. (20:9) [Is 40:30].
