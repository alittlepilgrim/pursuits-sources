### Psalm 56

### Trust in God

1 For the director. According to *Yonath elem rehoqim*. A *miktam* of David, when the Philistines seized him at Gath. ^psalm-56-1

#### I

2 Have mercy on me, God, ^psalm-56-2

for I am treated harshly;

attackers press me all the day.

3 My foes treat me harshly all the day; ^psalm-56-3

yes, many are my attackers.

O Most High,

4 when I am afraid, ^psalm-56-4

in you I place my trust.

5 I praise the word of God; ^psalm-56-5

I trust in God, I do not fear.

What can mere flesh do to me?

#### Ii

6 All the day they foil my plans; ^psalm-56-6

their every thought is of evil against me.

7 They hide together in ambush; ^psalm-56-7

they watch my every step;

they lie in wait for my life.

8 They are evil; watch them, God! ^psalm-56-8

Cast the nations down in your anger!

9 My wanderings you have noted; ^psalm-56-9

are my tears not stored in your flask,

recorded in your book?

10 My foes turn back when I call on you. ^psalm-56-10

This I know: God is on my side.

11 I praise the word of God, ^psalm-56-11

I praise the word of the L ORD.

12 In God I trust, I do not fear. ^psalm-56-12

What can man do to me?

#### Iii

13 I have made vows to you, God; ^psalm-56-13

with offerings I will fulfill them,

14 For you have snatched me from death, ^psalm-56-14

kept my feet from stumbling,

That I may walk before God

in the light of the living.

\* (Psalm 56) Beset physically ([Ps 56:2] – [3]) and psychologically ([Ps 56:6] – [7]), the psalmist maintains a firm confidence in God ([Ps 56:5], [9] – [10]). Nothing will prevent the psalmist from keeping the vow to give thanks for God’s gift of life ([Ps 56:13]). A refrain ([Ps 56:5], [11] – [12]) divides the Psalm in two equal parts.

\* (56:1) Yonath elem rehoqim: Hebrew words probably designating the melody to which the Psalm was to be sung.

\* (56:9) Are my tears not stored in your flask: a unique saying in the Old Testament. The context suggests that the tears are saved because they are precious; God puts a high value on each of the psalmist’s troubles.

b. (56:5) [Ps 130:5].

c. (56:5) [Ps 118:6]; [Heb 13:6].

d. (56:7) [Ps 140:5] – [6].

e. (56:9) ([[psalm-10|Ps 10]]:14]; [[2-kings-20|2 Kgs 20]]:5; [Is 25:8]; ([[revelation-07|Rv 7]]:17].

f. (56:13) [Nm 30:3].
