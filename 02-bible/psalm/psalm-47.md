### Psalm 47

### The Ruler of All the Nations

1 For the leader. A psalm of the Korahites. ^psalm-47-1

#### I

2 All you peoples, clap your hands; ^psalm-47-2

shout to God with joyful cries.

3 For the L ORD, the Most High, is to be feared, ^psalm-47-3

the great king over all the earth,

4 Who made people subject to us, ^psalm-47-4

nations under our feet,

5 Who chose our heritage for us, ^psalm-47-5

the glory of Jacob, whom he loves.

*Selah*

#### Ii

6 God has gone up with a shout; ^psalm-47-6

the L ORD, amid trumpet blasts.

7 Sing praise to God, sing praise; ^psalm-47-7

sing praise to our king, sing praise.

#### Iii

8 For God is king over all the earth; ^psalm-47-8

sing hymns of praise.

9 God rules over the nations; ^psalm-47-9

God sits upon his holy throne.

10 The princes of the peoples assemble ^psalm-47-10

with the people of the God of [[abraham|Abraham]].

For the shields of the earth belong to God,

highly exalted.

\* (Psalm 47) A hymn calling on the nations to acknowledge the universal rule of Israel’s God ([Ps 47:2] – [5]) who is enthroned as king over Israel and the nations ([Ps 47:6] – [9]).

\* (47:6) God has gone up: Christian liturgical tradition has applied the verse to the Ascension of Christ.
