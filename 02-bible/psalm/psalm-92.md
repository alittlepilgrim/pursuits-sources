# Psalm 92

## A Hymn of Thanksgiving for God’s Fidelity

1 A psalm. A sabbath song. ^psalm-92-1

### I

2 It is good to give thanks to the LORD, ^psalm-92-2

to sing praise to your name, Most High,

3 To proclaim your love at daybreak, ^psalm-92-3

your faithfulness in the night,

4 With the ten-stringed harp, ^psalm-92-4

with melody upon the lyre.

5 For you make me jubilant, LORD, by your deeds; ^psalm-92-5

at the works of your hands I shout for joy.

### II

6 How great are your works, LORD! ^psalm-92-6

How profound your designs!

7 A senseless person cannot know this; ^psalm-92-7

a fool cannot comprehend.

8 Though the wicked flourish like grass ^psalm-92-8

and all sinners thrive,

They are destined for eternal destruction;

9 but you, LORD, are forever on high. ^psalm-92-9

10 Indeed your enemies, LORD, ^psalm-92-10

indeed your enemies shall perish;

all sinners shall be scattered.

### III

11 You have given me the strength of a wild ox; ^psalm-92-11

you have poured rich oil upon me.

12 My eyes look with glee on my wicked enemies; ^psalm-92-12

my ears shall hear what happens to my wicked foes.

13 The just shall flourish like the palm tree, ^psalm-92-13

shall grow like a cedar of Lebanon.

14 Planted in the house of the LORD, ^psalm-92-14

they shall flourish in the courts of our God.

15 They shall bear fruit even in old age, ^psalm-92-15

they will stay fresh and green,

16 To proclaim: “The LORD is just; ^psalm-92-16

my rock, in whom there is no wrong.”

\* (Psalm 92) A hymn of praise and thanks for God’s faithful deeds ([Ps 92:2] – [5]). The wicked, deluded by their prosperity ([Ps 92:6] – [9]), are punished ([Ps 92:10]), whereas the psalmist has already experienced God’s protection ([Ps 92:11] – [15]).

\* (92:14) Planted: the just are likened to trees growing in the sacred precincts of the Temple, which is often seen as the source of life and fertility because of God’s presence, cf. [Ps 36:9], [10]; [Ez 47:1] – [12].
