### Psalm 23

### The Lord, Shepherd and Host

1 A psalm of David. ^psalm-23-1

#### I

The L ORD is my shepherd;

there is nothing I lack.

2 In green pastures he makes me lie down; ^psalm-23-2

to still waters he leads me;

3 he restores my soul. ^psalm-23-3

He guides me along right paths

for the sake of his name.

4 Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, ^psalm-23-4

I will fear no evil, for you are with me;

your rod and your staff comfort me.

#### Ii

5 You set a table before me ^psalm-23-5

in front of my enemies;

You anoint my head with oil;

my cup overflows.

6 Indeed, goodness and mercy will pursue me ^psalm-23-6

all the days of my life;

I will dwell in the house of the L ORD

for endless days.

\* (Psalm 23) God’s loving care for the psalmist is portrayed under the figures of a shepherd for the flock (([[psalm-23|Ps 23]]:1] – [4]) and a host’s generosity toward a guest (([[psalm-23|Ps 23]]:5] – [6]). The imagery of both sections is drawn from traditions of the exodus ([Is 40:11]; [49:10]; [Jer 31:10]).

\* (23:1) My shepherd: God as good shepherd is common in both the Old Testament and the New Testament ([Ez 34:11] – [16]; [Jn 10:11] – [18]).

\* (23:3) Right paths: connotes “right way” and “way of righteousness.”

\* (23:5) You set a table before me: this expression occurs in an exodus context in [Ps 78:19]. In front of my enemies: my enemies see that I am God’s friend and guest. Oil: a perfumed ointment made from olive oil, used especially at banquets ([Ps 104:15]; ([[matthew-26|Mt 26]]:7]; ([[luke-07|Lk 7]]:37], [46]; [Jn 12:2]).

\* (23:6) Goodness and mercy: the blessings of God’s covenant with Israel.

a. (23:1) [Ps 80:2]; [95:7]; [100:3]; [Dt 2:7].

b. (23:3) [Prv 4:11].

c. (23:4) [Jb 10:21] – [22]; [Is 50:10].

d. (23:5) [Ps 92:11].

e. (23:5) ([[psalm-16|Ps 16]]:5].

f. (23:6) ([[psalm-27|Ps 27]]:4].
