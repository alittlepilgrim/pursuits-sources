# Psalm 54

## Confident Prayer in Great Peril

1 For the leader. On stringed instruments. A *maskil* of David, ^psalm-54-1

2 when the Ziphites came and said to Saul, “David is hiding among us.” ^psalm-54-2

### I

3 O God, by your name save me. ^psalm-54-3

By your strength defend my cause.

4 O God, hear my prayer. ^psalm-54-4

Listen to the words of my mouth.

5 Strangers have risen against me; ^psalm-54-5

the ruthless seek my life;

they do not keep God before them.

*Selah*

### II

6 God is present as my helper; ^psalm-54-6

the Lord sustains my life.

7 Turn back the evil upon my foes; ^psalm-54-7

in your faithfulness, destroy them.

8 Then I will offer you generous sacrifice ^psalm-54-8

and give thanks to your name, LORD, for it is good.

9 Because it has rescued me from every trouble, ^psalm-54-9

and my eyes look down on my foes.

\* (Psalm 54) A lament in which the person under attack calls directly upon God for help ([Ps 54:3] – [5]). Refusing to despair, the psalmist hopes in God, who is active in history and just ([Ps 54:6] – [7]). The Psalm ends with a serene promise to return thanks ([Ps 54:8] – [9]).

\* (54:3) By your name: one is present in one’s name, hence God as revealed to human beings.
