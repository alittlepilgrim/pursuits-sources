# Psalm 38

## Prayer of an Afflicted Sinner

1 A psalm of David. For remembrance. ^psalm-38-1

### I

2 LORD, do not punish me in your anger; ^psalm-38-2

in your wrath do not chastise me!

3 Your arrows have sunk deep in me; ^psalm-38-3

your hand has come down upon me.

4 There is no wholesomeness in my flesh because of your anger; ^psalm-38-4

there is no health in my bones because of my sin.

5 My iniquities overwhelm me, ^psalm-38-5

a burden too heavy for me.

### II

6 Foul and festering are my sores ^psalm-38-6

because of my folly.

7 I am stooped and deeply bowed; ^psalm-38-7

every day I go about mourning.

8 My loins burn with fever; ^psalm-38-8

there is no wholesomeness in my flesh.

9 I am numb and utterly crushed; ^psalm-38-9

I wail with anguish of heart.

10 My Lord, my deepest yearning is before you; ^psalm-38-10

my groaning is not hidden from you.

11 My heart shudders, my strength forsakes me; ^psalm-38-11

the very light of my eyes has failed.

12 Friends and companions shun my disease; ^psalm-38-12

my neighbors stand far off.

13 Those who seek my life lay snares for me; ^psalm-38-13

they seek my misfortune, they speak of ruin;

they plot treachery every day.

### III

14 But I am like the deaf, hearing nothing, ^psalm-38-14

like the mute, I do not open my mouth,

15 I am even like someone who does not hear, ^psalm-38-15

who has no answer ready.

16 LORD, it is for you that I wait; ^psalm-38-16

O Lord, my God, you respond.

17 For I have said that they would gloat over me, ^psalm-38-17

exult over me if I stumble.

### IV

18 I am very near to falling; ^psalm-38-18

my wounds are with me always.

19 I acknowledge my guilt ^psalm-38-19

and grieve over my sin.

20 My enemies live and grow strong, ^psalm-38-20

those who hate me grow numerous fraudulently,

21 Repaying me evil for good, ^psalm-38-21

accusing me for pursuing good.

22 Do not forsake me, O LORD; ^psalm-38-22

my God, be not far from me!

23 Come quickly to help me, ^psalm-38-23

my Lord and my salvation!

\* (Psalm 38) In this lament, one of the Penitential Psalms (cf. [Ps 6]), the psalmist acknowledges the sin that has brought physical and mental sickness and social ostracism. There is no one to turn to for help; only God can undo the past and restore the psalmist.
