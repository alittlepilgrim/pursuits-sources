# Psalm 44

## God’s Past Favor and Israel’s Present Need

1 For the leader. A *maskil* of the Korahites. ^psalm-44-1

### I

2 O God, we have heard with our own ears; ^psalm-44-2

our ancestors have told us

The deeds you did in their days,

with your own hand in days of old:

3 You rooted out nations to plant them, ^psalm-44-3

crushed peoples and expelled them.

4 Not with their own swords did they conquer the land, ^psalm-44-4

nor did their own arms bring victory;

It was your right hand, your own arm,

the light of your face for you favored them.

5 You are my king and my God, ^psalm-44-5

who bestows victories on Jacob.

6 Through you we batter our foes; ^psalm-44-6

through your name we trample our adversaries.

7 Not in my bow do I trust, ^psalm-44-7

nor does my sword bring me victory.

8 You have brought us victory over our enemies, ^psalm-44-8

shamed those who hate us.

9 In God we have boasted all the day long; ^psalm-44-9

your name we will praise forever.

*Selah*

### II

10 But now you have rejected and disgraced us; ^psalm-44-10

you do not march out with our armies.

11 You make us retreat before the foe; ^psalm-44-11

those who hate us plunder us at will.

12 You hand us over like sheep to be slaughtered, ^psalm-44-12

scatter us among the nations.

13 You sell your people for nothing; ^psalm-44-13

you make no profit from their sale.

14 You make us the reproach of our neighbors, ^psalm-44-14

the mockery and scorn of those around us.

15 You make us a byword among the nations; ^psalm-44-15

the peoples shake their heads at us.

16 All day long my disgrace is before me; ^psalm-44-16

shame has covered my face

17 At the sound of those who taunt and revile, ^psalm-44-17

at the sight of the enemy and avenger.

### III

18 All this has come upon us, ^psalm-44-18

though we have not forgotten you,

nor been disloyal to your covenant.

19 Our hearts have not turned back, ^psalm-44-19

nor have our steps strayed from your path.

20 Yet you have left us crushed, ^psalm-44-20

desolate in a place of jackals;

you have covered us with a shadow of death.

21 If we had forgotten the name of our God, ^psalm-44-21

stretched out our hands to another god,

22 Would not God have discovered this, ^psalm-44-22

God who knows the secrets of the heart?

23 For you we are slain all the day long, ^psalm-44-23

considered only as sheep to be slaughtered.

### IV

24 Awake! Why do you sleep, O Lord? ^psalm-44-24

Rise up! Do not reject us forever!

25 Why do you hide your face; ^psalm-44-25

why forget our pain and misery?

26 For our soul has been humiliated in the dust; ^psalm-44-26

our belly is pressed to the earth.

27 Rise up, help us! ^psalm-44-27

Redeem us in your mercy.

\* (Psalm 44) In this lament the community reminds God of past favors which it has always acknowledged ([Ps 44:2] – [9]). But now God has abandoned Israel to defeat and humiliation ([Ps 44:10] – [17]), though the people are not conscious of any sin against the covenant ([Ps 44:18] – [23]). They struggle with being God’s special people amid divine silence; yet they continue to pray ([Ps 44:24] – [26]).

\* (44:11) You make us retreat: the corollary of [Ps 44:3]. Defeat, like victory, is God’s doing; neither Israel nor its enemies can claim credit ([Ps 44:23]).

\* (44:19) Our hearts have not turned back: Israel’s defeat was not caused by its lack of fidelity.

\* (44:20) A place of jackals: following Israel’s defeat and exile ([Ps 44:11] – [12]), the land lies desolate, inhabited only by jackals, cf. [[isaiah-13|Is 13]]:22; [Jer 9:10]; [10:22]. Others take *tannim* as “sea monster” (cf. [Ez 29:3]; [32:2]) and render: “you crushed us as you did the sea monster.”
