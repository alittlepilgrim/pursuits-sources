# Psalm 76

## God Defends Zion

1 For the leader; a psalm with stringed instruments. A song of Asaph. ^psalm-76-1

### I

2 Renowned in Judah is God, ^psalm-76-2

whose name is great in Israel.

3 On Salem is God’s tent, his shelter on Zion. ^psalm-76-3

4 There the flashing arrows were shattered, ^psalm-76-4

shield, sword, and weapons of war.

*Selah*

### II

5 Terrible and awesome are you, ^psalm-76-5

stronger than the ancient mountains.

6 Despoiled are the stouthearted; ^psalm-76-6

they sank into sleep;

the hands of all the men of valor have failed.

7 At your roar, O God of Jacob, ^psalm-76-7

chariot and steed lay still.

8 You, terrible are you; ^psalm-76-8

who can stand before you and your great anger?

9 From the heavens you pronounced sentence; ^psalm-76-9

the earth was terrified and reduced to silence,

10 When you arose, O God, for judgment ^psalm-76-10

to save the afflicted of the land.

*Selah*

11 Surely the wrath of man will give you thanks; ^psalm-76-11

the remnant of your furor will keep your feast.

### III

12 Make and keep vows to the LORD your God. ^psalm-76-12

May all around him bring gifts to the one to be feared,

13 Who checks the spirit of princes, ^psalm-76-13

who is fearful to the kings of earth.

\* (Psalm 76) A song glorifying Zion, the mountain of Jerusalem where God destroyed Israel’s enemies. Zion is thus the appropriate site to celebrate the victory ([Ps 76:3] – [4]), a victory described in parallel scenes ([Ps 76:5] – [7], [8] – [11]). Israel is invited to worship its powerful patron deity ([Ps 76:12]).

\* (76:3) Salem: an ancient name for Jerusalem, used here perhaps on account of its allusion to the Hebrew word for peace, *shalom*, cf. [[genesis-14|Gen 14]]:18; [Heb 7:1] – [3].

\* (76:5) Ancient mountains: conjectural translation of a difficult Hebrew phrase on the basis of [Gn 49:26]. The mountains are part of the structure of the universe ([Ps 89:12] – [13]).
