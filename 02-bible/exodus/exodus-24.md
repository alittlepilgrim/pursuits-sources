# Exodus, Chapter 24

## Chapter 24

**Ratification of the Covenant.**

1 Moses himself was told: Come up to the LORD, you and Aaron, with Nadab, Abihu, and seventy of the elders of Israel. You shall bow down at a distance. ^exodus-24-1

2 Moses alone is to come close to the LORD; the others shall not come close, and the people shall not come up with them. ^exodus-24-2

4 Moses then wrote down all the words of the LORD and, rising early in the morning, he built at the foot of the mountain an altar and twelve sacred stones for the twelve tribes of Israel. ^exodus-24-4

5 Then, having sent young men of the Israelites to offer burnt offerings and sacrifice young bulls as communion offerings to the LORD, ^exodus-24-5

6 Moses took half of the blood and put it in large bowls; the other half he splashed on the altar. ^exodus-24-6

7 Taking the book of the covenant, he read it aloud to the people, who answered, “All that the LORD has said, we will hear and do.” ^exodus-24-7

8 Then he took the blood and splashed it on the people, saying, “This is the blood of the covenant which the LORD has made with you according to all these words.” ^exodus-24-8

9 Moses then went up with Aaron, Nadab, Abihu, and seventy elders of Israel, ^exodus-24-9

10 and they beheld the God of Israel. Under his feet there appeared to be sapphire tilework, as clear as the sky itself. ^exodus-24-10

11 Yet he did not lay a hand on these chosen Israelites. They saw God, and they ate and drank. ^exodus-24-11

**Moses on the Mountain.**

12 The LORD said to Moses: Come up to me on the mountain and, while you are there, I will give you the stone tablets on which I have written the commandments intended for their instruction. ^exodus-24-12

13 So Moses set out with Joshua, his assistant, and went up to the mountain of God. ^exodus-24-13

14 He told the elders, “Wait here for us until we return to you. Aaron and Hur are with you. Anyone with a complaint should approach them.” ^exodus-24-14

15 Moses went up the mountain. Then the cloud covered the mountain. ^exodus-24-15

16 The glory of the LORD settled upon Mount Sinai. The cloud covered it for six days, and on the seventh day he called to Moses from the midst of the cloud. ^exodus-24-16

17 To the Israelites the glory of the LORD was seen as a consuming fire on the top of the mountain. ^exodus-24-17

18 But Moses entered into the midst of the cloud and went up on the mountain. He was on the mountain for forty days and forty nights. ^exodus-24-18

\* (24:4) Sacred stones: stone shafts or slabs, erected as symbols of the fact that each of the twelve tribes had entered into this covenant with God; see [23:24]; [Gn 28:18].

\* (24:11) They saw God: the ancients thought that the sight of God would bring instantaneous death. Cf. [33:20]; [[genesis-16|Gen 16]]:13; [32:31]; [Jgs 6:22] – [23]; [13:22]. Ate and drank: partook of the sacrificial meal.
