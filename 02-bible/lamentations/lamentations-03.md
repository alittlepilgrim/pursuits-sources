# Lamentations, Chapter 3

## Chapter 3

### The Voice of a Suffering Individual

1 I am one who has known affliction ^lamentations-03-1

under the rod of God’s anger,

2 One whom he has driven and forced to walk ^lamentations-03-2

in darkness, not in light;

3 Against me alone he turns his hand— ^lamentations-03-3

again and again all day long.

4 He has worn away my flesh and my skin, ^lamentations-03-4

he has broken my bones;

5 He has besieged me all around ^lamentations-03-5

with poverty and hardship;

6 He has left me to dwell in dark places ^lamentations-03-6

like those long dead.

7 He has hemmed me in with no escape, ^lamentations-03-7

weighed me down with chains;

8 Even when I cry for help, ^lamentations-03-8

he stops my prayer;

9 He has hemmed in my ways with fitted stones, ^lamentations-03-9

and made my paths crooked.

10 He has been a bear lying in wait for me, ^lamentations-03-10

a lion in hiding!

11 He turned me aside and tore me apart, ^lamentations-03-11

leaving me ravaged.

12 He bent his bow, and set me up ^lamentations-03-12

as a target for his arrow.

13 He pierced my kidneys ^lamentations-03-13

with shafts from his quiver.

14 I have become a laughingstock to all my people, ^lamentations-03-14

their taunt all day long;

15 He has sated me with bitterness, ^lamentations-03-15

filled me with wormwood.

16 He has made me eat gravel, ^lamentations-03-16

trampled me into the dust;

17 My life is deprived of peace, ^lamentations-03-17

I have forgotten what happiness is;

18 My enduring hope, I said, ^lamentations-03-18

has perished before the LORD.

19 The thought of my wretched homelessness ^lamentations-03-19

is wormwood and poison;

20 Remembering it over and over, ^lamentations-03-20

my soul is downcast.

21 But this I will call to mind; ^lamentations-03-21

therefore I will hope:

22 The LORD’s acts of mercy are not exhausted, ^lamentations-03-22

his compassion is not spent;

23 They are renewed each morning— ^lamentations-03-23

great is your faithfulness!

24 The LORD is my portion, I tell myself, ^lamentations-03-24

therefore I will hope in him.

25 The LORD is good to those who trust in him, ^lamentations-03-25

to the one that seeks him;

26 It is good to hope in silence ^lamentations-03-26

for the LORD’s deliverance.

27 It is good for a person, when young, ^lamentations-03-27

to bear the yoke,

28 To sit alone and in silence, ^lamentations-03-28

when its weight lies heavy,

29 To put one’s mouth in the dust— ^lamentations-03-29

there may yet be hope—

30 To offer one’s cheek to be struck, ^lamentations-03-30

to be filled with disgrace.

31 For the Lord does not ^lamentations-03-31

reject forever;

32 Though he brings grief, he takes pity, ^lamentations-03-32

according to the abundance of his mercy;

33 He does not willingly afflict ^lamentations-03-33

or bring grief to human beings.

34 That someone tramples underfoot ^lamentations-03-34

all the prisoners in the land,

35 Or denies justice to anyone ^lamentations-03-35

in the very sight of the Most High,

36 Or subverts a person’s lawsuit— ^lamentations-03-36

does the Lord not see?

37 Who speaks so that it comes to pass, ^lamentations-03-37

unless the Lord commands it?

38 Is it not at the word of the Most High ^lamentations-03-38

that both good and bad take place?

39 What should the living complain about? ^lamentations-03-39

about their sins!

40 Let us search and examine our ways, ^lamentations-03-40

and return to the LORD!

41 Let us lift up our hearts as well as our hands ^lamentations-03-41

toward God in heaven!

42 We have rebelled and been obstinate; ^lamentations-03-42

you have not forgiven us.

43 You wrapped yourself in wrath and pursued us, ^lamentations-03-43

killing without pity;

44 You wrapped yourself in a cloud, ^lamentations-03-44

which no prayer could pierce.

45 You have made us filth and rubbish ^lamentations-03-45

among the peoples.

46 They have opened their mouths against us, ^lamentations-03-46

all our enemies;

47 Panic and the pit have been our lot, ^lamentations-03-47

desolation and destruction;

48 My eyes stream with tears over the destruction ^lamentations-03-48

of the daughter of my people.

49 My eyes will flow without ceasing, ^lamentations-03-49

without rest,

50 Until the LORD from heaven ^lamentations-03-50

looks down and sees.

51 I am tormented by the sight ^lamentations-03-51

of all the daughters of my city.

52 Without cause, my enemies snared me ^lamentations-03-52

as though I were a bird;

53 They tried to end my life in the pit, ^lamentations-03-53

pelting me with stones.

54 The waters flowed over my head: ^lamentations-03-54

and I said, “I am lost!”

55 I have called upon your name, O LORD, ^lamentations-03-55

from the bottom of the pit;

56 You heard me call, “Do not let your ear be deaf ^lamentations-03-56

to my cry for help.”

57 You drew near on the day I called you; ^lamentations-03-57

you said, “Do not fear!”

58 You pleaded my case, Lord, ^lamentations-03-58

you redeemed my life.

59 You see, LORD, how I am wronged; ^lamentations-03-59

do me justice!

60 You see all their vindictiveness, ^lamentations-03-60

all their plots against me.

61 You hear their reproach, LORD, ^lamentations-03-61

all their plots against me,

62 The whispered murmurings of my adversaries, ^lamentations-03-62

against me all day long;

63 Look! Whether they sit or stand, ^lamentations-03-63

I am the butt of their taunt.

64 Give them what they deserve, LORD, ^lamentations-03-64

according to their deeds;

65 Give them hardness of heart; ^lamentations-03-65

your curse be upon them;

66 Pursue them in wrath and destroy them ^lamentations-03-66

from under the LORD’s heaven!

\* (3:1] – [66) This chapter is focused less on the destruction of Jerusalem than are chaps. [1] and [2] and more on the suffering of an individual. The identity of the individual is never given, and one probably should not search for a specific identification of the speaker. The figure of the representative sufferer makes concrete the pain of the people in a way similar to the personification of Zion as a woman in chaps. [1] and [2]. Indeed, in vv. [40] – [48] the individual voice gives way to a communal voice, returning in vv. [49] – [66] to the individual sufferer.

\* (3:21] – [24) In the midst of a description of suffering, the speaker offers this brief but compelling statement of hope in God’s ultimate mercy. It is a hard-won and precarious hope, nearly submerged by the volume and intensity of the surrounding lament, but it is hope nonetheless.

\* (3:29) To put one’s mouth in the dust: a sign of humiliation and submission; cf. v. [16]; [Ps 72:9].

\* (3:40] – [66) The plural voice in this lament suggests that a communal lament begins here; it then continues in the singular voice in vv. [55] – [66].

\* (3:48] – [51) These verses are more appropriate on the lips of the poet, who speaks of “my city” (v. [51]). Daughters of my city: here as elsewhere “daughter” may refer to villages dependent on a larger city.
