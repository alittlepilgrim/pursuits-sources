# Venial sin

## Catechism of the Catholic Church

Sin which does not destroy the divine life in the soul, as does mortal sin, though it diminishes and wounds it ([[3.1.1.8.p6c#^ccc-1855|1855]]). Venial sin is the failure to observe necessary moderation, in lesser matters of the moral law, or in grave matters acting without full knowledge or complete consent ([[3.1.1.8.p6c#^ccc-1862|1862]]).

## Divine Intimacy

- diminishes the vigor of charity and hinders its development: 101,1;
- when venial — is deliberate and habitual, it leads to tepidity 101,1; 153,1;
- venial — always includes a transgression more or less light of the law of God: 102,1;
- conduct in case of venial — caused by frailty or inadvertence: 101,2.
