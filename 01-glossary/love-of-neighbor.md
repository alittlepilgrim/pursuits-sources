# Love of Neighbor

## The Way of Perfection

LOVE FOR ONE ANOTHER. Three kinds are mentioned: excessive ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-6|4.6]]); mixed ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-12|4.12]]); and spiritual ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-12|4.12]]). The latter two are virtuous involving a friendship based on likeness ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-15|4.15]]). In excessive love, one is dominated by an affection based on natural graces ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-7|4.7]]). Virtuous love is directed toward helping one another love God more ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-6|4.6]]). Spiritual love is a virtuous love that does not stir our feelings of affection; mixed love is a virtuous love accompanied by feelings of affection ([[tj-wop-chapter-4#^wop-ch4-12|4.12]]). She decides not to discuss mixed love ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-7|7.7]]). Few have the purely spiritual love; it is characteristic of the highest perfection ([[tj-wop-chapter-6#^wop-ch6-1|6.1]]). It arises not from thinking or believing but from a clear experiential knowledge of Creator and creature ([[tj-wop-chapter-6#^wop-ch6-3|6.3]]). It understands that God is its origin ([[tj-wop-chapter-6#^wop-ch6-8|6.8]]). It entrusts to God the care of those from whom love is received ([[tj-wop-chapter-6#^wop-ch6-5|6.5]]). It has no self-interest, will strive to relieve others of work, rejoices and praises God for any increase of virtue it sees in the other ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-9|7.9]]), sets aside its own advantage for the other's sake ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-8|7.8]]); all it wants is to see the other rich with heavenly blessings ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-1|7.1]]–[[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-2|2]]). It would rather suffer a trial itself than see the other suffer it, but if it sees that the other grows spiritually through its trial it rejoices ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-3|7.3]]). It cannot be insincere with those who are loved ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-4|7.4]]). It knows how to suffer the fault of another and not be surprised and strives to practice better the opposite virtue ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-7|7.7]]). It helps very much to further peace in a community ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-9|7.9]]). It knows how to take recreation with others when needed ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-7|7.7]]). It becomes impassioned in its care to see the other make progress toward God and not turn back ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-1|7.1]]). It imitates the love that Jesus has for us ([[tj-wop-chapter-7#^wop-ch7-4|7.4]]). It is impossible to hide this love ([[tj-wop-chapter-40#^wop-ch40-7|40.7]]). We must strive to be affable, agreeable, and pleasing to those with whom we deal ([[tj-wop-chapter-41#^wop-ch41-7|41.7]]).
