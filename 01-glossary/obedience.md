# Obedience

## Catechism of the Catholic Church

The submission to the authority of God which requires everyone to obey the divine law. Obedience to the Church is required in those things which pertain to our salvation; and obedience is due to legitimate civil authority, which has its origin in God for the sake of the common good and the order of society ([[3.1.2.2.p6j#^ccc-1897|1897]]). The fourth commandment obliges children to obey their parents ([[3.2.2.4.p7u#^ccc-2216|2216]]).

(2) _Obedience of faith_: The first obedience is that of faith: to listen and freely submit to the word of God ([[1.1.3.1.pv#^ccc-144|144]]).

(3) _Obedience of Christ_: Jesus Christ substituted his obedience to the will of his Father, even unto death, for the disobedience of sin, in order to bring us the grace of justification and to satisfy for our sins ([[1.2.2.4.p1o#^ccc-615|615]]).

(4) _Vow of obedience_: In imitation of this obedience of Jesus, as an evangelical counsel, the faithful may profess a vow of obedience; a public vow of obedience, accepted by Church authority, is one element that characterizes the consecrated life ([[1.2.3.9.p2a#^ccc-915|915]]).
