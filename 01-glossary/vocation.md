# Vocation

## Catechism of the Catholic Church

The calling or destiny we have in this life and hereafter. God has created the human person to love and serve him; the fulfillment of this vocation is eternal happiness ([[00.prologue-1#^ccc-1|1]], [[1.2.1.1.6.p1b#^ccc-358|358]], [[3.1.1.0.p5f#^ccc-1700|1700]]). Christ calls the faithful to the perfection of holiness ([[1.2.3.9.p29#^ccc-825|825]]). The vocation of the laity consists in seeking the Kingdom of God by engaging in temporal affairs and directing them according to God's will ([[1.2.3.9.p2a#^ccc-898|898]]). Priestly and religious vocations are dedicated to the service of the Church as the universal sacrament of salvation (cf. [[1.2.3.9.p2a#^ccc-873|873]]; [[1.2.3.9.p2a#^ccc-931|931]]).
