# Life

## Catechism of the Catholic Church

Both God's gift of created human life and His divine life given to us as sanctifying grace. Beyond its ordinary meaning of human life, Jesus used "life" to signify a share in his own _divine_ Trinitarian existence, which becomes possible for those who respond to his invitation to turn away from sin and open their hearts to God's abiding love. _Eternal_ life signifies that this gift will last forever in the blessedness of heaven. This gift of God begins with the "life" of faith and "new life" of Baptism ([[2.2.1.0.p3i#^ccc-1225|1225]]), is communicated in sanctifying grace ([[3.1.3.2.p6z#^ccc-1997|1997]]), and reaches perfection in the communion of life and love with the Holy Trinity in heaven ([[1.2.3.12.p2m#^ccc-1023|1023]]).
