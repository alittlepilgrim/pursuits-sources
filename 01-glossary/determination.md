# Determination

## The Way of Perfection

DETERMINATION. A firmness of resolve associated with perseverance ([[tj-wop-chapter-21#^wop-ch21-2|21.2]]). The object of it is to continue until reaching the end ([[tj-wop-chapter-21#^wop-ch21-2|21.2]]). The determination includes a resolve to die rather than give up the journey ([[tj-wop-chapter-20#^wop-ch20-2|20.2]]). The Lord highly favors those who have real determination ([[tj-wop-chapter-14#^wop-ch14-1|14.1]]). We must give ourselves to the Lord with the kind of determination with which he gives himself to us ([[tj-wop-chapter-16#^wop-ch16-9|16.9]]). It is important to begin with determination; the person who does so struggles more courageously ([[tj-wop-chapter-23#^wop-ch23-1|23.1]]; [[tj-wop-chapter-23#^wop-ch23-5|23.5]]). The devil is afraid of determined souls, but will cause fears and never-ending obstacles to those who are changeable, unstable, and not strongly determined to persevere ([[tj-wop-chapter-23#^wop-ch23-4|23.4]]). The more determination we have not to commit an offense against God for any creature, the less confidence we have in ourselves and the more in God ([[tj-wop-chapter-41#^wop-ch41-4|41.4]]).
