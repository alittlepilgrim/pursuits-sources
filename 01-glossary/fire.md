# Fire

## The Way of Perfection

FIRE. A symbol for the love of God enkindled in the soul by the living water of contemplation ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.2]]–[[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|3]]). This water of contemplation makes the fire of God's love increase in the soul ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.3]]). This fire has its source in God, not in anything on earth, and cannot be extinguished by the water of earthly things when the fire is powerful ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.4]]; [[chapter-40-tj-way-ccel|40.4]])). Earthly things can extinguish the fire when it is weak ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.4]]). There is another heavenly water given by God in prayer—the water of tears—that does not quench the heavenly fire of God's love but makes it grow ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.5]]). The fire of God's love cools and freezes all worldly attachments ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.5]]). It communicates itself to others, and wants to burn up the whole world ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.5]]). With contemplatives the fire shines brightly and so their love is clearly recognized ([[chapter-40-tj-way-ccel|40.4]]).

In the prayer of recollection, the soul is well prepared for the enkindling of fire; blowing a little with the intellect can hasten the enkindling ([[chapter-28-tj-way-ccel|28.8]]).

Fire is also a symbol for God; the soul will be warmed by approaching the fire; when one receives Communion, or makes a spiritual communion, and wants to get warm, it will stay warm for many hours afterward ([[chapter-35-tj-way-ccel|35.1]]).
