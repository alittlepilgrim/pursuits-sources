## The Way of Perfection

DETACHMENT. Relinquishing what stands in the way of giving oneself to the All without reserve ([[chapter-08-tj-way-ccel|8.1]]; [[chapter-32-tj-way-ccel|32.9]]); in it one embraces the Creator rather than the creature, cares not for what comes to an end but for eternal things ([[chapter-08-tj-way-ccel|8.1]]; [[chapter-32-tj-way-ccel|32.9]]; [[chapter-03-tj-way-ccel|3.4]]). Outward detachment is necessary until one gains from the Lord a freedom in regard to the attachment ([[chapter-08-tj-way-ccel|8.4]]; [[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|10.1]]). The freedom from attachment gives one dominion over all creation ([[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|10.3]]). An aid to detachment is the thought of how quickly everything comes to an end ([[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|10.2]]). Along with humility, it is a virtue loved by Christ, who was never seen without it ([[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|10.3]]). Through the two virtues, one escapes from Egypt and finds the true manna ([[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|10.4]]). These two virtues cannot be present without love for others ([[chapter-16-tj-way-ccel|16.2]]). The objects of detachment include ourselves, our bodies, and our honor ([[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|10.5]]; [[chapter-12-tj-way-ccel|12.5]]).
