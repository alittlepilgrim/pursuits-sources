## The Way of Perfection

UNION (PRAYER OF). A “very supernatural” prayer in which the entire soul is engulfed in God; all the faculties are suspended (held bound) by God ([[tj-wop-chapter-31#^wop-ch31-10|31.10]]; [[tj-wop-chapter-42#^wop-ch42-5|42.5]]). As one who never tires of giving, God begins to take delight in such a soul, reveal his secrets, and rejoice that it knows something of what he will give it; God even makes it lose the exterior senses in rapture so that nothing will occupy it ([[tj-wop-chapter-32#^wop-ch32-12|32.12]]). God takes joy in putting the soul in command and does what the soul asks since it does his will ([[tj-wop-chapter-32#^wop-ch32-12|32.12]]). The king of glory will not come to our soul if we do not make the effort to gain the great virtues ([[tj-wop-chapter-16#^wop-ch16-6|16.6]]). A refusal to forgive is a sure sign of the absence of this prayer of union; an effect of the prayer is a readiness to forgive offenders immediately and to remain on very good terms with them ([[tj-wop-chapter-36#^wop-ch36-12|36.12]]).
