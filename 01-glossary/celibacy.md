# Celibacy

## Catechism of the Catholic Church

The state or condition of those who have chosen to remain unmarried for the sake of the kingdom of heaven in order to give themselves entirely to God and to the service of his people. In the Latin Church, celibacy is obligatory for bishops and priests. In some Eastern Churches, celibacy is a prerequisite for the ordination only of bishops; priests may not marry after they have been ordained ([[2.2.3.6.p4x#^ccc-1579|1579]], [[2.2.3.6.p4x#^ccc-1580|1580]]).
