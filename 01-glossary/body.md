# Body

## The Way of Perfection

BODY. The external part of the human composite (body and soul) ([[chapter-31-tj-way-ccel|31.2]]–[[chapter-31-tj-way-ccel|3]]). Its senses or powers are mainly those of sight and hearing ([[chapter-28-tj-way-ccel|28.5]]–[[chapter-28-tj-way-ccel|6]]; [[chapter-31-tj-way-ccel|31.2]]; [[chapter-34-tj-way-ccel|34.12]]), which cannot on their own perceive the Lord, truly present in the Blessed Sacrament ([[chapter-34-tj-way-ccel|34.5]]), or when he speaks to the heart ([[chapter-24-tj-way-ccel|24.5]]). The more comforts the body receives, the more needs it discovers ([[chapter-11-tj-way-ccel|11.2]]); its senses can be a source of distraction ([[chapter-28-tj-way-ccel|28.5]]); thus it can become an enemy in the spiritual battle of this life ([[chapter-11-tj-way-ccel|11.5]]). The body should be put to work for one's daily sustenance ([[chapter-34-tj-way-ccel|34.4]]). In sickness it should be cared for, and compassion should be shown toward the sick ([[chapter-11-tj-way-ccel|11.1]]). The devil tempts people toward bodily penances that can ruin their health ([[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|19.9]]–[[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|10]]). In the prayer of quiet the body experiences rest and delight ([[chapter-31-tj-way-ccel|31.2]]–[[chapter-31-tj-way-ccel|3]]). In rapture, awareness through the exterior senses is lost ([[chapter-32-tj-way-ccel|32.12]]).
