# Vocal Prayer

## The Way of Perfection

VOCAL PRAYER. Recitation of a prayer formula such as the Our Father or the Hail Mary ([[chapter-25-tj-way-ccel|25.3]]); but we must refuse to be satisfied with merely pronouncing the words ([[chapter-24-tj-way-ccel|24.2]]; [[chapter-22-tj-way-ccel|22.2]]). The mind and heart should be aware of what we are saying and to whom we are speaking ([[chapter-21-tj-way-ccel|21.10]]; [[chapter-24-tj-way-ccel|24.5]]–[[chapter-24-tj-way-ccel|6]]), centered on the one to whom the words are addressed ([[chapter-24-tj-way-ccel|24.6]]). Vocal prayer, then, must not be separated from mental prayer ([[chapter-22-tj-way-ccel|22.3]]). Many persons while praying vocally are raised by God to sublime contemplation ([[chapter-30-tj-way-ccel|30.7]]; [[chapter-25-tj-way-ccel|25.1]]). In the liturgy, when in the Creed the words “and his kingdom will have no end,” were said, it was almost always a special delight for Teresa ([[chapter-22-tj-way-ccel|22.1]]); when we begin to recite the Hours consider whom we are going to speak with and who we are ([[chapter-22-tj-way-ccel|22.3]]).
