# Vocal Prayer

## The Way of Perfection

VOCAL PRAYER. Recitation of a prayer formula such as the Our Father or the Hail Mary ([[tj-wop-chapter-25#^wop-ch25-3|25.3]]); but we must refuse to be satisfied with merely pronouncing the words ([[tj-wop-chapter-24#^wop-ch24-2|24.2]]; [[tj-wop-chapter-22#^wop-ch22-2|22.2]]). The mind and heart should be aware of what we are saying and to whom we are speaking ([[tj-wop-chapter-21#^wop-ch21-10|21.10]]; [[tj-wop-chapter-24#^wop-ch24-5|24.5]]–[[tj-wop-chapter-24#^wop-ch24-6|6]]), centered on the one to whom the words are addressed ([[tj-wop-chapter-24#^wop-ch24-6|24.6]]). Vocal prayer, then, must not be separated from mental prayer ([[tj-wop-chapter-22#^wop-ch22-3|22.3]]). Many persons while praying vocally are raised by God to sublime contemplation ([[tj-wop-chapter-30#^wop-ch30-7|30.7]]; [[tj-wop-chapter-25#^wop-ch25-1|25.1]]). In the liturgy, when in the Creed the words “and his kingdom will have no end,” were said, it was almost always a special delight for Teresa ([[tj-wop-chapter-22#^wop-ch22-1|22.1]]); when we begin to recite the Hours consider whom we are going to speak with and who we are ([[tj-wop-chapter-22#^wop-ch22-3|22.3]]).
