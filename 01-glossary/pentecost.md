# Pentecost

## Catechism of the Catholic Church

The "fiftieth" day at the end of the seven weeks following Passover (Easter in the Christian dispensation). At the first Pentecost after the Resurrection and Ascension of Jesus, the Holy Spirit was manifested, given and communicated as a divine Person to the Church, fulfilling the paschal mystery of Christ according to his promise ([[1.2.3.8.p23#^ccc-726|726]], [[1.2.3.8.p24#^ccc-731|731]]; cf. [[2.2.1.2.p3q#^ccc-1287|1287]]). Annually the Church celebrates the memory of the Pentecost event as the beginning of the new "age of the Church," when Christ lives and acts in and with his Church ([[2.1.0.0.p2u#^ccc-1076|1076]]).
