# Essays on Woman Chapter 1 Glossary

- **Anima Forma Corporis:** A Thomistic principle meaning "the soul is the form of the body." The text uses this to argue that distinct spiritual characteristics must exist to correspond to distinct physical forms (e.g., feminine vs. masculine). ^0d9ffb
- **Companion Gift:** Woman's natural faculty to empathetically share in the life of another human being, showing active sympathy, and taking part in their joys, sufferings, work, and problems. ^e0ba04
- **Ethos:** In its enduring sense, an inner form, a constant spiritual attitude (habitus) operative within a person, which possesses positive value and satisfies objective requirements. It shapes changing behavioral patterns and manifests in external action. ^149ce9
- **Eucharistic Life:** A life centered on and nourished by the Holy Eucharist, involving daily, confidential relationship with the Lord in the tabernacle, self-forgetting love, and an open heart to the needs of others. ^2fd609
- **Feminine Professions (objective tasks):** Vocations whose objective requirements are harmonious with feminine nature, often depending on sympathetic rapport. Examples include nursing, education, social work, and branches of scholarship dealing with the concrete, living personal element. ^830fcc
- **Grace:** The divine influence which liberates nature from its flaws, restores it to purity, and enables it to receive divine life, acting from within. ^3b5ea5
- **Habitus:** A constant spiritual attitude or basic disposition of the soul. Examples include temperament (inborn), acquired proficiencies and virtues, and divine virtues (established habitus). ^849603
- **Handmaid of the Lord:** A reference to the Virgin Mary, symbolizing a woman's complete surrender to God's will and faithful service, regardless of her circumstances. ^c061f1
- **Liturgical Life:** A life formed by praying together with the Church, indicating a deep participation in the Church's communal worship and spiritual practices. ^fdc8e6
- **Maternal Gift:** Woman's natural yearning and inclination to cherish, guard, protect, nourish, and advance growth in the living, personal, and whole. ^3ccb49
- **Natural Vocation:** A calling or purpose that aligns with one's inherent nature. For woman, this is specifically identified as being wife and mother, for which she is physically and spiritually endowed. ^18a5d4
- **Objective Work (as remedy):** Engaging in tasks that demand repression of an excessively personal attitude, an end to superficiality, and submission to objective laws, thereby schooling one in obedience and fostering maturity. ^9d6778
- **Perversion (of Feminine Nature):** The negative manifestations of a fallen feminine nature, resulting from original sin. Examples include vanity, gossip, superficiality, an unchecked need for communication, and a dominating will. ^6826b1
- **Professional Ethos:** The abiding spiritual attitude or totality of habitus that emerges from within as the formative principle in a person's professional life, giving it a definite uniform character not imposed externally. ^380d93
- **Supernatural Vocation:** A calling from God that raises a human being above the natural earthly level, such as the religious vocation involving total surrender to God. ^d5cfa7
