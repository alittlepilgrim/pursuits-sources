## Introduction: Ethos, Professional Ethos

- **Ethos**
    - Relation to human action
    - Inner form / spiritual attitude (habitus)
    - Types of habitus
        - Temperament (inborn)
        - Acquired (proficiencies, virtues)
        - Established (divine virtues)
    - Focus on positive values / objective requirements
- **Professional Ethos**
    - Abiding spiritual attitude
    - Formative principle in professional life
    - Emerges from within, not imposed
    - Examples: Loyalty, conscientiousness
    - Determines results (vocation vs. income)
    - Peculiar to each profession's purpose
- **Precluded Suppositions (to be discussed)**
    - Attitudes unique to feminine soul
    - Woman's nature draws her to certain professions

## Woman's Natural Vocation and Corresponding Ethos

- Is there a specific feminine vocation?
    - Points to recurrent ideal
    - Opposes any easy, static execution
- Woman's Created Purpose
    - With particular (biological, psychological)
    - Emotional emphasis with spirituality
    - Differing type and direction (from men's principles)
- Fundamentals of Feminine Spiritual Attitude
    - Seeks living contact
    - Seeks concrete reality (body, spirit, mind, creation, growth)
    - Lifelong love more than fleeting
    - Association rather than being alone
    - Inclines to and cultivates personal
    - Instinctive and psychological sympathy
- Maternal Gift: Jesus' 1st Comparison
    - Shares life of another
    - Empathic for every circumstance
    - Active sympathy toward person, happenings, achievements
- Substantial and Objective Purpose in Life
    - Directed by God's word
    - Not motivated by pride
    - Corresponds to all-embracing human vocation
- Family-Oriented Feminine Vocation (Guides the World)
    - Essential for social structure
    - Not identical in form: specific circumstances
    - Engagement toward duties
    - Vocation directs to concrete circumstances
    - Implies new development: leads to progress
    - Typical mission: thorough completion of duties for the Gospel
- Wider Ranges (Original and Specific Circumstances)
    - Sent as servant
    - God appoints
    - May range to Christ, Life, Souls (Ends/Goods)
- Impact of New Times on Mother's Vocation
    - New responsibilities
    - Selfless sacrifice and attention
    - Natural sacrifices, often unnoticed
    - No love as deep, no faith as indestructible
    - Woman's nature: directed toward man & God
- Accepting Suffering Ethos
    - Multiplicity: family responsibilities
    - Limitations of mind, related things (great transformations)

## Other Natural Feminine Vocations

- Women's Capability Beyond Spouse/Mother
    - Experience demonstrates ability to hold positions
    - Individual gift/talents for diverse activities (artistic,
        scientific, technical)
- Significantly Feminine Professions (Objective Tasks)
    - Depend on sympathetic rapport
    - Examples: Nursing, education, social work, doctor, teacher,
        governess, housemaid
    - Scholarship: concrete, bring personal element (arts,
        translating, editing)
    - Same spiritual attitude as wife/mother; extended to wider circle
    - Requires greater self-sacrifice
- Professions Not Harmonious with Feminine Nature (Masculine)
    - Can be practiced in authentically feminine way
    - Adjustment to dull material/abstract thought (factory, office,
        lab)
    - Opportunity for feminine virtues among others
    - Blessed counterbalance to mechanizations of humanity
    - View of the whole; abstract serves the living
    - Woman seeks concrete goal, adjusts means to end (Parliament,
        administration)
- Blessing for Society
    - Women's participation in diverse disciplines
    - Preserving specifically feminine ethos
- Prototype: Mary at Wedding of Cana
    - Quiet, observing, discovers lacking
    - Procures remedy, gives directive quietly
    - Performs work dutifully, vigilantly
    - Conscious of want, intervenes discreetly
    - Spreads blessing like a good spirit

## The Supernatural Vocation of Woman

- Religious Vocation as Feminine Vocation
    - Lord calls men and women for holy service
    - Supernatural, transcends natural earthly level
    - Grace perfects nature, doesn't destroy it
    - Masculine and feminine nature fitted uniquely
- Elements of Religious Vocation
    - Total surrender of self and life to God
    - Renunciation of possessions, human ties, own will
    - Diverse service
- Activities by Sex in Religious Orders
    - Contemplative liturgy transcends sex
    - Spreading God's charity: more active (also women in teaching
        orders)
    - Charitable tasks: delicate receptivity, decisively feminine
    - Older Orders: more spiritual intimacy, service over appearance
        (Sacrificing/Redemptive), youth work
    - Contemporary women's communities: educational and charitable
    - External efficacy similar to women in the world
    - Difference: religious life performed in spirit of obedience/for
        God
- Religious Life & Feminine Nature (Absolute Gift of Self to God)
    - Motive, principle, end: self-surrender to God
    - God's love: overflowing, merciful, healing, protecting, teaching
    - Deepest longing of woman's heart: gives self lovingly, belongs
        possessio
    - Only God can confer satisfaction, answer full complexity
    - Total surrender: only adequate fulfillment for woman's yearning
    - Divine life entering corresponds to woman's professional ethos
- Practical Consequences
    - Not all women must become religious
    - Fallen feminine nature: weakness, but complete surrender to God
    - Everywhere: a "handmaid of the Lord" (Mother of God as model)
    - Image of Mother of God = ultimate feminine vocation

## Challenges and Solutions for Women Today

- Actual Life of Contemporary Women
    - Contradictory situation: labor force/family
    - Double vocation, double burden (profession & home)
    - Specific conflicts: unstable situation
        - Child education difficult
        - Job obligations, work organization
        - Ambiguities between vocation and job
        - Not all jobs compatible with family
        - Influence of outside factors: job market, state, economy
        - Contradicts true nature of woman
- Changes for Men
    - Woman in closer profession
    - Teacher for good of family
    - Direction of home and school
- Broader Strength of Women
    - True function: not only job & home, even more important
    - Nation needs working women today, integrated into professions
    - Restoring society, saving culture, shaping Christian future
    - Professional life needs active Christian women
- Elevating Power of Ethos
    - Woman more fit for absolute
    - Can elevate striving for good, general ethos
- Increasing Dignities
    - Council reasserted woman's dignity (example: Council)
    - Economic life
    - Lay apostolate, works outside common order
    - Authority within society, works of culture
    - Woman as decisive in Church/society foundations
- Struggles
    - Woman's situation: not entirely favorable
    - Must be given possibility, right conditions
    - If not: high quality of service, ethos will fail
    - Woman's ethos a blessing: essential for Church/society
- Summary of Author's View/Intention
    - Woman's situation presents two levels
    - Natural feminine vocation cannot and should not be altered
    - Supernatural vocation: no compromise
    - Vocation ethos safeguards woman

## Concluding Questions

- Why special category for women's professions?
- Why frequent discussions on women's but not men's professions?
- Man's nature as co-determining factor for vocation?
- Antithesis: nature degenerated by Fall vs. restored purity (for man
    too?)

**Goal:**

- Wholesome collaboration of sexes in professional life
- Achieved through calm, objective awareness of nature
- God created humanity as man and woman in His image
- Purely developed masculine and feminine nature yields highest
    likeness to God
- Strongest interpenetration of earthly and divine life
