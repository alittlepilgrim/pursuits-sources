# The Way of Perfection by St. Teresa of Ávila (CCEL Edition)

[Source](https://ccel.org/ccel/t/teresa/way/cache/way.txt)

## Publication Info

Title: The Way of Perfection Creator(s): Teresa of Avila, St. (Teresa de Cepeda y Ahumada,1515-1582) CCEL Subjects: All; Classic; Mysticism; LC Call no: BX2179 LC Subjects: Christian Denominations Roman Catholic Church Meditations. Devotional readings. Spiritual exercises, etc.

[[tj-way-ccel-intro|Edition Notes, Abbreviations and Introduction]]

[[chapter-01-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 1. Of the Reason Which Moved Me to Found This Convent in Such Strict Observance]]

[[chapter-02-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 2. Treats of How the Necessities of the Body Should Be Disregarded and of the Good That Comes from Poverty]]

[[chapter-03-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 3. Continues the Subject Begun in the First Chapter and Persuades the Sisters to Busy Themselves Constantly in Beseeching God to Help Those Who Work for the Church. Ends with an Exclamatory Prayer]]

[[chapter-04-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 4. Exhorts the Nuns to Keep Their Rule and Names Three Things Which Are Important for the Spiritual Life. Describes the First of These Three Things, Which Is Love of One's Neighbour, and Speaks of the Harm Which Can Be Done by Individual Friendships]]

[[chapter-05-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 5. Continues Speaking of Confessors. Explains Why It Is Important That They Should Be Learned Men]]

[[chapter-06-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 6. Returns to the Subject of Perfect Love, Already Begun]]

[[chapter-07-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 7. Treats of the Same Subject of Spiritual Love and Gives Certain Counsels for Gaining It]]

[[chapter-08-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 8. Treats of the Great Benefit of Self-detachment, Both Interior and Exterior, from All Things Created]]

[[chapter-09-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 9. Treats of the Great Blessing That Shunning Their Relatives Brings to Those Who Have Left the World and Shows How by Doing so They Will Find Truer Friends]]

[[chapter-10-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 10. Teaches That Detachment from the Things Aforementioned Is Insufficient if We Are Not Detached from Our Own Selves and That This Virtue and Humility Go Together]]

[[chapter-11-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 11. Continues to Treat of Mortification and Describes How It May Be Attained in Times of Sickness]]

[[chapter-12-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 12. Teaches That the True Lover of God Must Care Little for Life and Honour]]

[[chapter-13-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 13. Continues to Treat of Mortification and Explains How One Must Renounce the World's Standards of Wisdom in Order to Attain to True Wisdom]]

[[chapter-14-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 14. Treats of the Great Importance of Not Professing Anyone Whose Spirit Is Contrary to the Things Aforementioned]]

[[chapter-15-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 15. Treats of the Great Advantage Which Comes from Our Not Excusing Ourselves, Even Though We Find We Are Unjustly Condemned]]

[[chapter-16-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 16. Describes the Difference Between Perfection in the Lives of Contemplatives and in the Lives of Those Who Are Content with Mental Prayer. Explains How It Is Sometimes Possible for God to Raise a Distracted Soul to Perfect Contemplation and the Reason for This. This Chapter and That Which Comes Next Are to Be Noted Carefully]]

[[chapter-17-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 17. How Not All Souls Are Fitted for Contemplation and How Some Take Long to Attain It. True Humility Will Walk Happily Along the Road by Which the Lord Leads It]]

[[chapter-18-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 18. Continues the Same Subject and Shows How Much Greater Are the Trials of Contemplatives than Those of Actives. This Chapter Offers Great Consolation to Actives]]

[[chapter-19-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 19. Begins to Treat of Prayer. Addresses Souls Who Cannot Reason with the Understanding]]

[[chapter-20-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 20. Describes How, in One Way or Another, We Never Lack Consolation on the Road of Prayer. Counsels the Sisters to Include This Subject Continually in Their Conversation]]

[[chapter-21-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 21. Describes the Great Importance of Setting out upon the Practice of Prayer with Firm Resolution and of Heeding No Difficulties Put in the Way by the Devil]]

[[chapter-22-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 22. Explains the Meaning of Mental Prayer]]

[[chapter-23-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 23. Describes the Importance of Not Turning Back When One Has Set out upon the Way of Prayer. Repeats How Necessary It Is to Be Resolute]]

[[chapter-24-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 24. Describes How Vocal Prayer May Be Practised with Perfection and How Closely Allied It Is to Mental Prayer]]

[[chapter-25-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 25. Describes the Great Gain Which Comes to a Soul When It Practises Vocal Prayer Perfectly. Shows How God May Raise It Thence to Things Supernatural]]

[[chapter-26-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 26. Continues the Description of a Method for Recollecting the Thoughts. Describes Means of Doing This. This Chapter Is Very Profitable for Those Who Are Beginning Prayer]]

[[chapter-27-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 27. Describes the Great Love Shown Us by the Lord in the First Words of the Paternoster and the Great Importance of Our Making No Account of Good Birth if We Truly Desire to Be the Daughters of God]]

[[chapter-28-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 28. Describes the Nature of the Prayer of Recollection and Sets Down Some of the Means by Which We Can Make It a Habit]]

[[chapter-29-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 29. Continues to Describe Methods for Achieving This Prayer of Recollection. Says What Little Account We Should Make of Being Favoured by Our Superiors]]

[[chapter-30-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 30. Describes the Importance of Understanding What We Ask for in Prayer. Treats of These Words in the Paternoster: “santificetur Nomen Tuum, Adveniat Regnum tuum.” Applies Them to the Prayer of Quiet, and Begins the Explanation of Them]]

[[chapter-31-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 31. Continues the Same Subject. Explains What Is Meant by the Prayer of Quiet. Gives Several Counsels to Those Who Experience It. This Chapter Is Very Noteworthy]]

[[chapter-32-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 32. Expounds These Words of the Paternoster: “fiat Voluntas Tua Sicut in Coelo Et in terra.” Describes How Much Is Accomplished by Those Who Repeat These Words with Full Resolution and How Well the Lord Rewards Them for It]]

[[chapter-33-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 33. Treats of Our Great Need That the Lord Should Give Us What We Ask in These Words of the Paternoster: “panem Nostrum Quotidianum Da Nobis hodie.”]]

[[chapter-34-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 34. Continues the Same Subject. This Is Very Suitable for Reading After the Reception of the Most Holy Sacrament]]

[[chapter-35-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 35. Describes the Recollection Which Should Be Practiced After Communion. Concludes This Subject with an Exclamatory Prayer to the Eternal Father]]

[[chapter-36-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 36. Treats of These Words in the Paternoster: “dimitte Nobis Debita nostra.”]]

[[chapter-37-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 37. Describes the Excellence of This Prayer Called the Paternoster, and the Many Ways in Which We Shall Find Consolation in It]]

[[chapter-38-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 38. Treats of the Great Need Which We Have to Beseech the Eternal Father to Grant Us What We Ask in These Words: “et Ne Nos Inducas in Tentationem, Sed Libera Nos a malo.” Explains Certain Temptations. This Chapter Is Noteworthy]]

[[chapter-39-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 39. Continues the Same Subject and Gives Counsels Concerning Different Kinds of Temptation. Suggests Two Remedies by Which We May Be Freed from Temptations]]

[[chapter-40-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 40. Describes How, by Striving Always to Walk in the Love and Fear of God, We Shall Travel Safely Amid All These Temptations]]

[[chapter-41-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 41. Speaks of the Fear of God and of How We Must Keep Ourselves from Venial Sins]]

[[chapter-42-tj-way-ccel|Chapter 42. Treats of These Last Words of the Paternoster: “sed Libera Nos a Malo. amen.” “but Deliver Us from Evil. amen.”]]
