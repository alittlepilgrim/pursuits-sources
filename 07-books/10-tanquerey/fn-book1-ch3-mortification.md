[2] Since mortification is defined as the struggle against our evil inclinations, it must be practiced first of all in resisting temptations. This aspect of mortification will be treated in nos 900 and following. It is next practiced in overcoming our evil inclinations, our vices. This will be seen in nos 818 and following. Here we speak only of the mortification of our faculties, or rather of their inordinate tendencies.

It must be noted that the word mortification is not used in exactly the same sense when we speak of the mortification of our sins and vices as when we speak of the mortification of our faculties. In the former case it means destroying, putting to death; in the latter it means correcting, training, disciplining.

[3] To resume the practices of corporal mortification is one of the most effective means of regaining lost joy of spirit and fervor of soul: “Let us go back to our bodily mortifications. If the Saints are such gay spirits, and monks and nuns such unaccountably cheerful creatures, it is simply because their bodies, like St. Paul’s, are chastised and kept under with an unflinching sharpness and a vigorous discretion.” (FABER, The Blessed Sacrament, Book II, Section VII).
